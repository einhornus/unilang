огнево́й (5423)


Etymology

ого́нь (ogónʹ) +‎ -евой (-evój). ого́нь (ogónʹ) comes ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₁n̥gʷnis, whence English igneous (“containing, resembling or produced by fire”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ɐɡnʲɪˈvoj]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Adjective

огнево́й • (ognevój)

  1. (relational) fire
    огнева́я мощь ― ognevája moščʹ ― firepower
    огнева́я заве́са ― ognevája zavésa ― curtain of fire (literally, “fire curtain”)
    огнева́я то́чка ― ognevája tóčka ― weapon emplacement (literally, “fire point”)
    огнево́й рубе́ж ― ognevój rubéž ― firing line
  2. fiery, passionate
  3. fervent, ardent, boisterous, perky, full of life/pep

Related terms

  • ого́нь (ogónʹ)
  • о́гненный (ógnennyj)
