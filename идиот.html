идио́т -> idiot
Noun, declension 2a (pattern ма́льчик)

Plural: идио́ты
Genitive: идио́та



Etymology: First attested in 1803. Borrowed from Latin idiōta via French idiot or German Idiot, from Ancient Greek ἰδιώτης (idiṓtēs, “a private citizen, one who has no professional knowledge, layman”).



Definitions (English):
  • idiot.
    • Ты тако́й идио́т. ― Ty takój idiót. ― You're such an idiot.
