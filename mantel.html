From Middle English">Middle English mantel, from Old English ang">mentel (“sleeveless cloak”), later reinforced by cognate Anglo-Norman fro">mantel, both from Latin mantēllum (“covering, cloak”), diminutive of mantum, from Celtiberian *mantum, from a Proto-Indo-European root shared with Old Norse non">mǫttull.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈmæn.təl/
  • Homophones: mantle
  • Rhymes: -æntəl

Noun

mantel (plural mantels)

  1. The shelf above a fireplace which may be also a structural support for the masonry of the chimney.
  2. (climbing) A maneuver to surmount a ledge, involving pushing down on the ledge to bring up the body. Also called a mantelshelf.
