тесный -> tight
Adjective



Etymology: From Old East Slavic тѣснъ (těsnŭ), from Proto-Slavic *těsnъ (“narrow”). According to Václav Machek, it is related to Ancient Greek στενός (stenós, “narrow, tight”), thus making both derive from Proto-Indo-European *ténh₂us (“thin”).



Definitions (English):
  • tight, close, narrow
    • small,
      • мир те́́сен ― mir tésen ― it's a small world
    • (short form only) too tight, too narrow (of clothing and shoes)
      • мне э́ти штаны́́ те́́сны ― mne éti štaný tésny ― these trousers are too tight for me
