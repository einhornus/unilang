олень (3002)


Etymology

From Proto-Slavic *(j)elenь, Proto-Indo-European *h₁el-(h₁)en- (“red deer, elk”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₁el- (“red deer, elk”), whence English elk. Cognate with Ancient Greek ἑλλός (hellós, “deer-calf”), ἔλαφος (élaphos, “deer”), Lithuanian elnias, Classical Armenian">Old Armenian եղն (ełn).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ɐˈlʲenʲ]
  • Audio

  • Rhymes: -enʲ

Noun

олень • (olénʹ) masculine gender">m anim (genitive оле́ня, nominative plural оле́ни, genitive plural оле́ней)

  1. deer
    северный олень ― sévernyj olénʹ ― reindeer
    америка́нский северный олень ― amerikánskij sévernyj olénʹ ― caribou
    безрогий олень ― bezrógij olénʹ ― pollard
    благоро́дный олень ― blagoródnyj olénʹ ― stag, red deer, elk (U.S.) (Cervus elaphus)
    настоя́щий олень ― nastojáščij olénʹ ― stag, red deer, elk (U.S.) (Cervus elaphus)
  2. buck, stag (male deer)
  3. (colloquial, derogatory) dimwit, fool, narrow-minded individual
