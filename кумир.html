куми́р -> idol
Noun, declension 2a (pattern ма́льчик)

Plural: куми́ры
Genitive: куми́ра



Etymology: Borrowed from Old Church Slavonic кѹмиръ (kumirŭ), whose origin is uncertain. Perhaps a TurkicΏ] or Semiticΐ] borrowing: compare Classical Syriac ܟܘܡܪܐ‎ (kūmrāʾ, “priest”), whence Old Armenian քուրմ (kʿurm, “priest”). Has also been connected to Ossetian гуымиры (g°ymiry, “giant”) and Georgian გმირი (gmiri, “hero”) and together with them derived from the name of Cimmerians.



Definitions (English):
  • (paganism) idol, graven image
    • (figuratively) beloved person
