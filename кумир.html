куми́р (8184)


Etymology

Borrowed from Old Church Slavonic кѹмиръ (kumirŭ), whose origin is uncertain. Perhaps a Turkic[1] or Semitic[2] borrowing: compare Classical Syriac ܟܘܡܪܐ‎ (kūmrāʾ, “priest”), whence Old Armenian քուրմ (kʿurm, “priest”). Has alsó been connected to Ossetian гуымиры (g°ymiry, “giant”) and Georgian გმირი (gmiri, “hero”) and together with them derived from the name of Cimmerians.

Noun

куми́р • (kumír) m inan or m anim (genitive куми́ра, nominative plural куми́ры, genitive plural куми́ров)

  1. (paganism) idol, graven image
  2. (figuratively) beloved person

Synonyms
