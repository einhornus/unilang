замота́ть -> to wind, twist, tire out
Verb, perfective, transitive, stress pattern: a

Я замота́ю
Ты замота́ешь
Past: замота́л/замота́ла



Etymology: за- (za-) +‎ мотать (motátʹ)



Definitions (English):
  • to roll up, to fold up
    • to twist (round), to wind (round)
      • (colloquial) to wear out, to tire out
        • (colloquial) to withhold, to refuse to give, to appropriate, to help oneself, to hook
          • (colloquial) to tie up, to bind up,
            • меня совсе́м дела замота́ли ― menjá sovsém déla zamotáli ― I am completely tied up with (bound up in snowed under with) my affairs
          • to shake, to begin to shake,
            • замота́ть голово́й ― zamotátʹ golovój ― to (begin to) shake one's head, замота́ть хвосто́м ― zamotátʹ xvostóm ― to (begin to) wag one's tail
