pact (0)


Etymology

From Middle French pacte, from Old French, from Latin pactum (“something agreed upoń”), from paciscere (“to agree”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /pækt/
  • Rhymes: -ækt
  • Homophone: packed

Noun

pact (plural pacts)

  1. An agreement; a compact; a covenant.
  2. (international law) An agreement betweeń two or more nations

Verb

pact (third-person singular simple present pacts, present participle pacting, simple past and past participle pacted)

  1. (intransitive) To form a pact; to agree formally.
