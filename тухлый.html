ту́хлый (13716)


Etymology

Inherited from Proto-Slavic *tǫxlъ, also found as Proto-Slavic *tъxlъ (as in dialectal тохлый (tóxlyj, “rotten and foul-smelling”)), which may represent an earlier variant. Compare тухнуть (túxnutʹ, “to become rotten and foul-smelling”), Belarusian тхліць (txlicʹ, “to cause to spoil”), тухнуць (túxnucʹ, “to become rotten and foul-smelling”), Slovene zatȯhǝl (“giving off dampness”) (tonal orthography), otȯhlica (“stuffiness”) (tonal orthography), otȯhniti se (“to spoil”) (tonal orthography), Czech stuchlý (“stale, rotten”), tuchnouti (“to fade”), stuchlý (“stale, rotten”), Polish language">Polish stęchły (“musty”), tęchnąć (“to become rotten and foul-smelling”), Upper Sorbian language">Upper Sorbian tuchły (“rotten and foul-smelling”), tuchnyć (“to become rotten and foul-smelling”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈtuxlɨj]

Adjective

ту́хлый • (túxlyj) (comparative (по)тухлее or (по)тухлей)

  1. rotten and foul-smelling
  2. (colloquial) musty, stale, stagnant (of water and air)

Synonyms

  • (stale): за́тхлый (zátxlyj), несве́жий (nesvéžij)

Related terms

  • тухнуть impf (túxnutʹ), поту́хнуть pf (potúxnutʹ)
