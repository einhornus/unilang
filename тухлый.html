ту́хлый (13716)


Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Proto-Slavic *tǫxlъ, also found as Proto-Slavic *tъxlъ (as in dialectal тохлый (tóxlyj, “rotten and foul-smelling”)), which may represent an earlier variant. Compare тухнуть (túxnutʹ, “to become rotten and foul-smelling”), Belarusian тхліць (txlicʹ, “to cause to spoil”), тухнуць (túxnucʹ, “to become rotten and foul-smelling”), Slovene zatȯhǝl (“giving off dampness”) (tonal orthography), otȯhlica (“stuffiness”) (tonal orthography), otȯhniti se (“to spoil”) (tonal orthography), Czech stuchlý (“stale, rotten”), tuchnouti (“to fade”), stuchlý (“stale, rotten”), Polish stęchły (“musty”), tęchnąć (“to become rotten and foul-smelling”), Upper Sorbian tuchły (“rotten and foul-smelling”), tuchnyć (“to become rotten and foul-smelling”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ˈtuxlɨj]

Adjective[edit]

ту́хлый • (túxlyj) (comparative (по)тухлее or (по)тухлей)

  1. rotten and foul-smelling
  2. (colloquial) musty, stale, stagnant (of water and air)

Synonyms[edit]

  • (stale): за́тхлый (zátxlyj), несве́жий (nesvéžij)

Related terms[edit]

  • тухнуть impf (túxnutʹ), поту́хнуть pf (potúxnutʹ)
