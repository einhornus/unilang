хата́ -> peasant house
Noun, declension 1a (pattern девочка)

Plural: хаты
Genitive: хаты



Etymology: Borrowed from Ukrainian ха́та́ (xáta, “house home”).



Definitions (English):
  • (a peasant's) house, hut,
    • пойдём в ха́ту́ ― pojdjóm v xátu ― let's go inside (literally, “let's go into the house”)
  • (slang) house, joint, a place one can booze or have sex,
    • вчера́́ мы буха́ли у ко́реша на ха́те́ ― včerá my buxáli u kóreša na xáte ― yesterday/last night we boozed at pal's place (note: preposition "на" is used in this sense)
  • ( Ukraine, Southern Russia) home,
    • моя́ ха́та́ с кра́ю, я ничего́́ не зна́ю ― mojá xáta s kráju, ja ničevó ne znáju ― none of my business (literally, “my home is on the edge (of the village), I don't know anything”)
  • (slang) prison cell
    • mistake
