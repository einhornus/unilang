потрепа́ть -> to rumple, tousle, pat, (coll) natter
Verb, perfective, transitive, stress pattern: c

Я потреплю́
Ты потре́плешь
Past: потрепа́л/потрепа́ла



Etymology: по- (po-) +‎ трепа́ть (trepátʹ)



Definitions (English):
  • to pull about, to blow about, to flutter, to tousle
    • to pat,
      • потрепа́ть кого́-либо по плечу́ ― potrepátʹ kovó-libo po plečú ― to pat smb's shoulder
    • to beat up, to maul, to pummel,
      • сосе́ди потрепа́ли во́ра ― sosédi potrepáli vóra ― neighbours mauled the thief
    • to give a hard life/time,
      • жизнь потрепа́ла его́ ― žiznʹ potrepála jevó ― he had a life full of trouble his troubles had told on his appearance
    • to mention too often, to make too many references (to), to hackney,
      • не хо́чется потрепа́ть э́то высо́кое сло́во ― ne xóčetsja potrepátʹ éto vysókoje slóvo ― I wouldn't like to use this lofty word without a special reason, после э́той исто́рии его́ и́мя ста́ли потрепа́ть вовсю ― pósle étoj istórii jevó ímja stáli potrepátʹ vovsjú ― after that incident, his name became the talk of the town
