бревно́ -> log, beam
Noun, declension 2d
Plural: брёвна
Genitive: бревна



Etymology: From Old East Slavic бьрьвьно (bĭrĭvĭno), from Proto-Slavic *bьrvьno.



Definitions (English):
  • log, beam (trunk of dead tree, cleared of branches)
    • Александр Солженицын, Матренин двор 1968:посерели от ста́рости брёвна сруба и ворота, когда-то могу́чие, и проредилась их обвершка, poseréli ot stárosti brjóvna srúba i vórota, kogdá-to mogúčije, i proredílasʹ ix obvérškathe frame beams as the door, once mighty, had grown with much time grey and its roof had weakened
  • (in the plural) lumber
    • (gymnastics) beam, balance beam
      • (derogatory) stupid or heartless man
        • (vulgar, derogatory) dead fish, cold fish (a very passive sexual partner)
