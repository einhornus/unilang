тулуп -> sheepskin coat
Noun, declension 2a (pattern мальчик)

Plural: тулупы
Genitive: тулупа



Etymology: Possibly from Turkic or some other Altaic language. The exact origin and its jaunt, per Vasmer, is hard to ascertain given that the borrowing is found in other Slavic languages such as Ukrainian ту́́луб (túlub), Belarusian ту́́лу́п (túlup). A more distant cognate is Turkish tulum.



Definitions (English):
  • sheepskin coat, overcoat, tulup ( Russian-style long warm sheepskin/hareskin coat with a collar)
