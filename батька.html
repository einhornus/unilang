батька -> dad, father (coll)
Noun, declension 1a
Plural: батьки
Genitive: батьки



Etymology: From ба́тя (bátja) +‎ -ка (-ka), originally a diminutive. Cognates include Ukrainian ба́тько (bátʹko), Belarusian ба́цька (bácʹka).



Definitions (English):
  • (colloquial) father, dad,
    • не ле́зь поперёд ба́тьки в пе́кло ― ne lezʹ poperjód batʹki v peklo ― don't jump the gun haste makes waste
  • (colloquial) "father", boss, master, lord, kingpin, number one, pro
    • (colloquial) a nickname for president of Belarus Alyaksandr Lukashenka, also the Belarusian term
      • ба́цька (bácʹka) is used in this sense.
    • genitive singular of ба́тько (bátʹko)
      • accusative singular of ба́тько (bátʹko)
        • genitive singular of батько́ (batʹkó)
          • accusative singular of батько́ (batʹkó)
