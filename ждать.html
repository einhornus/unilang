ждать -> to wait (for)
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: b/c

Я жд
Ты ждёшь
Past: ждл/жда́л



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *žьdati (“to wait, to await”), from Proto-Balto-Slavic *g(e)id-, from Proto-Indo-European *gʰidʰ-. Compare Old High German gīt (“avarice”) and Old Prussian gēide (“they are waiting”). Ώ].

  • to wait, to wait for (+ genitive case)1955, Vladimir Nabokov, chapter 14, in Lolita:

Vmésto tovó, štóby ždatʹ do ijúlja, kak býlo naméčen snačála. Lolita, it was decided, would be sent there on Thursday instead of waiting until July, as was initially planned. Ждать конца́ войны.Ždatʹ koncá vojný. To wait for the end of the war.
  • to expect, to await
