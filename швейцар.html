швейца́р (7801)


Etymology

Borrowed from German Schweizer (“Swiss person; doorman”) (because Swiss people were often employed as doormen), possibly via Polish language">Polish szwajcar. First used in the 18th century.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ʂvʲɪjˈt͡sar]

Noun

швейца́р • (švejcár) masculine gender">m anim (genitive швейца́ра, nominative plural швейца́ры, genitive plural швейца́ров)

  1. doorman (doorkeeper, usher, porter)
  2. (obsolete) Swiss man
