маля́вка (31108)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *maljavъka (compare with Belarusian маляўка (maljaŭka), Polish dialectal malawka, Ukrainian маля́вка (maljavka)), from *maljava +‎ *-ъka whence Russian dialectal малява (maljáva, “fry”) (also, compare with Czech malavý, Macedonian малав (malav), Serbo-Croatian malava), further from *malъ (“small; little”) +‎ *-ava, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *(s)mel-.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [mɐˈlʲafkə]

Noun[edit]

маля́вка • (maljávka) f anim (genitive маля́вки, nominative plural маля́вки, genitive plural маля́вок)

  1. (colloquial) whitebait
  2. (colloquial) little one (a young child); shorty, shrimp (a short person)

Synonyms[edit]

  • (whitebait): малёк (maljók)

Related terms[edit]

  • ма́лый (mályj)
