коле́но -> knee
Noun, declension 2a (pattern ле́то)

Plural: коле́нья
Genitive: коле́на



Etymology: From Old East Slavic колѣно (kolěno), from Proto-Slavic *kolěno. Cognate with Ukrainian коліно (kolino), Bulgarian коляно (koljano), Slovene koléno, Czech koleno, Polish kolano compare Lithuanian kelis, Ancient Greek κῶλον (kôlon).



Definitions (English):
  • (anatomy) knee
    • (in the plural) lap
      • bend (in a river, etc.)
        • (music) figure, part, passage, turn (in music or dance)
          • generation, branch
            • (biblical) tribe,
              • коле́но Даново ― koléno Dánovo ― the tribe of Dan
            • (engineering) joint, elbow, crank (pipes, tubing, rods)
              • (botany) joint, node
