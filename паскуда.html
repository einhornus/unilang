паску́да (23773)


Etymology

Cognate with Ukrainian паску́д (páskud), паску́да (paskúda), Belarusian поскудзь (póskudzʹ), Old East Slavic паскудьнъ (paskudĭnŭ, “poor”) (compare modern Russian паску́дный (paskúdnyj, “low, mean”)). Synchronically derivable from па- (pa-) +‎ скуд(ный) (skúd(nyj)) +‎ -а (-a).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [pɐˈskudə]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

паску́да • (paskúda) m anim or f anim (genitive паску́ды, nominative plural паску́ды, genitive plural паску́д)

  1. (colloquial, derogatory) a vile person, bastard, scoundrel, asshole

Noun

паску́да • (paskúda) f inan (genitive паску́ды, nominative plural паску́ды, genitive plural паску́д)

  1. (dated or regional) filth, abomination
