носк -> sock, toe of boot
Noun, declension 2b (pattern язк)

Plural: носк
Genitive: *



Etymologies:
  • Likely from нос (nos, “nose”) +‎ -ок (-ók) compare etymology 2. Sense development is "little nose" -> "projection" -> "toe" -> "sock". Less likely from носи́ть (nosítʹ, “to wear”) +‎ -ок (-ók).
  • нос (nos) +‎ -ок (-ók)


Definitions (English):
  • sock,
    • надева́ть носки́ ― nadevátʹ noskí ― to put on socks
  • toe (of a boot, shoe or sock)
    • toe (of a foot or hoof especially the tip of the toe)
      • приподнима́ться на носки́ ― pripodnimátʹsja na noskí ― to rise on one's toes
    • projection, nose (e.g. of a plane)
      • Diminutive of нос (nos, “nose”)
        • genitive plural of носка́ (nóska)
          • short masculine singular of ноский (nóskij)
