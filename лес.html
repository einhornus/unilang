лес -> wood, forest
Noun, declension 2c (pattern го́род)

Plural: леса
Genitive: леса



Etymology: From Old East Slavic лѣсъ (lěsŭ), from Proto-Slavic *lěsъ.

    • лес в плота́х ― lés v plotáx ― timber float, лес на ко́рню ― lés na kornjú ― standing timber
  • (only plural)
    • леса scaffolding, scaffold trestle, falsework, леса ― lesá ― scaffolding, scaffold trestle
  • (figuratively) much, many, a lot of smth. (especially high, eminent)
    • лес ру́к ― les ruk ― a forest of hands (in school if nobody wants to answer the teacher’s question, it's used sarcastically, when nobody actually raises a hand, there is no "forest of hands", not a single "tree", i.e. hand)лес ма́чт ― les mačt ― a large number of ships, лес штыко́в ― les štykóv ― forest of bayonets
