балахо́н (14426)


Etymology

Unclear. Formerly unsuccessfully derived from Persian بالا جامه‎ (bālā jāme, “upper dress”) or Turkic. May be related to балахтать (baláxtatʹ, “splash”), балахтаться (baláxtatʹsja, “gad”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [bəlɐˈxon]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

балахо́н • (balaxón) m inan (genitive балахо́на, nominative plural балахо́ны, genitive plural балахо́нов)

  1. (historical) loose peasant overgarment made of sackcloth or twill
  2. (colloquial) loose-fitting robe
