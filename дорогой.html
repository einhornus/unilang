дорогой (1394)


Etymology 1

From Proto-Slavic *dorgъ. Doublet of дра́гой (dragój), a borrowing from Old Church Slavonic.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [dərɐˈɡoj]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Adjective

дорогой • (dorogój) (comparative (по)дороже, superlative дорожайший)

  1. dear
  2. expensive
Usage notes
  • In the sense of "my dear", this word is not restricted to use betweeń men and women, but is quite common betweeń friends of the same sex, or a teacher to any of his students without́ regard to sex. When used betweeń men, мой дорогой (moj dorogoj) usually is not translated, since English does not possess a good unisex counterpart to this.
Related terms
  • до́рого (dórogo)
  • дорогови́зна (dorogovízna)
  • дорогуша (dorogúša)
  • дорожа́ть (dorožátʹ)
  • подорожание (podorožánije)
  • дорогосто́ящий (dorogostójaščij)
