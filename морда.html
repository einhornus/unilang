мо́рда -> snout, muzzle, (coll) face
Noun, declension 1a (pattern де́вочка)

Plural: мо́рды
Genitive: мо́рды



Etymologies:
  • Per Vasmer, from an Iranian language, based on the appearance of /r/ in place of /l/ cognate with Old English molda (“head”), Ancient Greek βλωθρός (blōthrós, “lifted up, high”), Sanskrit मूर्धन् (mūrdhán, “forehead, head, apex”), Avestan 𐬐𐬀-𐬨𐬆𐬭𐬆𐬜𐬀‎ (ka-mərəδa, “head”). See Proto-Indo-Iranian *mr̥Hdʰā. Possibly connected with synonymous Armenian մռութ (mṙutʿ).


Definitions (English):
  • face of an animal, muzzle, snout,
    • мо́рда ко́шки ― mórda kóški ― cat snout
  • (colloquial, derogatory) face of a person (usually ugly), mug
