каранда́ш -> pencil
Noun, declension 2b (pattern язы́к)

Plural: карандаши́
Genitive: карандаша́



Etymology: In Russian from the 16–17th centuries. Borrowing from a Turkic language. Compare Turkish karataş (“black slate”), composed of kara (“black”) + taş (“stone”), Tatar ка́ра таш (qara taş, “graphite, black lead”). The origin of epenthetic -н- (-n-) is unclear. Perhaps it was added in colloquial Russian speech to ease the pronunciation. Compare каланча (kalančá) for the same epenthesis.
