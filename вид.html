вид -> view, look, species
Noun, declension 2a (pattern мальчик)

Plural: виды
Genitive: вида



Etymology: From Old East Slavic видъ (vidŭ), from Proto-Slavic *vidъ, from Proto-Indo-European *weyd- (“to know see”). Cognate with Lithuanian veidas (“face”), Latvian veids (“form, shape”), Sanskrit वेद (veda, “knowledge”), Avestan 𐬬𐬀𐬉𐬛𐬀𐬵‎ (vaēdah-, “possession”), Latin videō (“to see”), Ancient Greek εἶδος (eîdos, “form, shape”), Old Irish fíad, Welsh gŵydd (“presence”) (from Proto-Celtic *weido-), and Old English witan (whence English wit).
