не́нависть -> hate
Noun, declension 3a (pattern жизнь)

Plural: не́нависти
Genitive: не́нависти



Etymology: From Old East Slavic не́нависть (nenavistĭ), from Proto-Slavic *nenavistь, from *nenaviděti + *-tь, from *ne (“not”) + *naviděti, from *na (“on(to), in(to)”) + *viděti (“to see”).

V sérdce málʹčika vméste s ljubóvʹju k otcú roslá nénavistʹ k tem, kto jevó preslédoval, zakovál v kandalý, soslál na kátorgu. In the boy’s heart, along with love for his father, there grew a hatred against those who persecuted him, bound him in chains, and banished him to Siberia.
