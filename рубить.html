руби́ть -> to chop
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: c

Я рублю́
Ты ру́бишь
Past: руби́л/руби́ла



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *rǫbiti. Compare Belarusian рубаць (rubácʹ), Bulgarian ръбя (rǎbja), Ukrainian рубити (rubýty), Czech roubit, Polish rąbać



Definitions (English):
  • to axe, to fell, to chop, to cut (with an axe)
    • (colloquial, figuratively) to not mince words, to speak the truth, to speak one's mind.
      • Он лю́бит руби́ть правду-матку. On ljúbit rubítʹ právdu-mátku. He doesn't like to mince words.(literally, “He likes to cut the mother truth.”)
