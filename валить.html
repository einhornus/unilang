валть -> to topple, heap up
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: c

Я вал
Ты влишь
Past: валл/валла



Etymology: Cognates include Latin volvō.



Definitions (English):
  • to knock down, to kill (e.g. of a disease killing people or animals)
    • Уста́лость меня про́сто ва́лит (с ног). Ustálostʹ menjá prósto válit (s nog). The fatigue is simply knocking me down.
  • (colloquial) to heap up (transitive)
    • Не надо вали́ть всё в одну́ ку́чу. Ne nádo valítʹ vsjó v odnú kúču. No need to lump everything together.
  • to cut, to fell (trees), to make someone fall, to throw down (on the ground) Perfectives:
    • повалить (povalítʹ), свали́ть (svalítʹ)
  • (colloquial) to push the blame or responsibility on someone else.
    • Не вали́ на друго́го, е́сли сам винова́т. Ne valí na drugóvo, jésli sam vinovát. Don't blame the other guy, if it's your own fault.
  • to throng (e.g. of people)
    • Наро́д ва́лит (то́лпами) на но́вый фильм. Naród valít (tólpami) na nóvyj filʹm. People are coming in hordes to the new film.
  • to fall heavily (e.g. of snow)
    • to belch (e.g. of smoke)
      • (colloquial) to go away, to scram.
        • Он по́нял, что пора́ вали́ть из страны. On pónjal, što porá valítʹ iz straný. He understood that it was time to get the heck out of the country. Perfective: свали́ть (svalítʹ)
