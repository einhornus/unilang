кабала́ -> slavery, servitude
Noun, declension 1b
Plural: кабалы́
Genitive: кабалы́



Etymologies:
  • Inherited from Old East Slavic кабала́ (kabala, “document, deed, debt issue”), from Turkish kabal (“certain quantity, tasked labour, one day labour”).
  • Borrowed from Hebrew קַבָּלָה‎‎ (“receiving/tradition”).


Definitions (English):
  • (historical) promissory note, leading to debt bondage if unpaid
    • (historical) debt bondage
      • enslavement, servitude, bondage
        • (religion) kabbalah
