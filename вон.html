вон -> out, away, over there

Etymology: Inherited from Old East Slavic вънъ (vŭnŭ). Cognate with dialectal во́нки (vónki, “out”), Old Church Slavonic вънъ (vŭnŭ, “out”), Ukrainian во́нка (vónka, “out”), Bulgarian вън (vǎn, “out”) (also въ́нка (vǎ́nka)), Serbo-Croatian ва̏н (“out”), Slovene vèn (“out”) (tonal orthography), Czech ven (“out”), Slovak von (“out”), Old Polish wen (“out”), Upper Sorbian won (“out”), Lower Sorbian wen (“out”). See вне (vne) for further discussion.



Definitions (English):
  • (informal) there.
    • Вон та́́м ― Von tám ― over there
  • off, out (away), outside, away.
    • Он вы́шёл вон. On výšel von. He went out (and away).
  • out, get out!
    • пошёл вон! ― pošól von! ― get out! get away from me!вон (оно́́) что! ― von (onó) što! ― You don’t say!
  • genitive plural of во́на (vóna)
