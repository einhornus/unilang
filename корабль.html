кора́бль -> ship
Noun, declension 2b (pattern язы́к)

Plural: корабли́
Genitive: корабля́



Etymology: From Old East Slavic кора́бль (korablĭ), from Proto-Slavic *korabľь, old borrowing from Ancient Greek καράβιον (karábion) or κάραβος (kárabos), whence English caravel.



Definitions (English):
  • ship, liner,
    • спи́сывать/списа́ть с корабля́ ― spísyvatʹ/spisátʹ s korabljá ― to transfer/post from a ship
  • (formally) warship
    • (architecture) nave
      • (figuratively) match box for marijuana, by consonance with коро́бок (korobok)
