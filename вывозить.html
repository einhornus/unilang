вы́возить (8932)


Etymology 1

From вы- (vý-) +‎ вози́ть (vozítʹ); by rule, вы- attracts the stress in perfective but not imperfective verbs.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [vɨvɐˈzʲitʲ]

Verb

вы́возить • (vyvozítʹ) impf (perfective вы́везти)

  1. to take out (by vehicle), to remove
    вы́возить на та́чке (pejorative)
    vyvozítʹ na táčke
    to protest by removing someone in a wheelbarrow from an administrative post
    (literally, “to cart awaý in a wheelbarrow”)
  2. to deliver
    вы́возить в свет (dated, high-style)
    vyvozítʹ v svet
    to introduce (young girls) to the community
    (literally, “to deliver intó the light”)
  3. to export
  4. to bring back
  5. (colloquial) to rescue, to save
Synonyms
  • (export): экспорти́ровать (eksportírovatʹ)
  • (rescue): выруча́ть (vyručátʹ)
Related terms
  • вы́воз (vývoz)
  • вывозка (vývozka)
  • вывозной (vyvoznój)

Etymology 2

See вы́возить (vývozitʹ) above.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈvɨvəzʲɪtʲ]

Verb

вы́возить • (vývozitʹ) pf

  1. (colloquial) to soil, to bedraggle
Synonyms
  • выпачкать (výpačkatʹ), вы́мазать (výmazatʹ), (colloquial) извозюкать (izvozjukatʹ)
