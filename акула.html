аку́ла (9369)


Etymology

Directly attested since 18th century as аккула (ákkula) and аку́л (akúl), while derivative акулий (akulij) is used to describe liver oil of a Greenland shark in a source going back to late 16th or early 17th century.

Immediate borrowing from rather different Old Norse non">hákarl (see internal etymology there) is rejected by Anikin, he rather proposes mediation by some languages">Sami language.

Compare dialectal Norwegian haakall, Sami language">Skolt Sami sms">akkli, Kildin Sami а̄һклэӈӈк (āhkleŋŋk), Northern Sami language">Northern Sami áhkạlakkis (“Greenland shark”).
