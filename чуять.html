чуять -> to scent, smell, sense, feel
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: a

Я чую
Ты чуешь
Past: чуял/чуяла



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *čùti, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kewh₁- (“to perceive”), whence English show and caution, as well as German schauen (“to look”), thereby English anschauung and weltanschauung (“sense of the world, worldview”).



Definitions (English):
  • to smell
    • to feel, to sense.
      • Я нутро́м чу́ю, не к добру́́ всё э́то. Ja nutróm čúju, ne k dobrú vsjó éto. I have a gut feeling (lit. I feel with [my] inside), this won't mean anything good.
    • (regional, colloquial) to hear
