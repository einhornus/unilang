Etymologies:
  • From Old East Slavic спѣти (spěti, “to hasten, to succeed”), from Proto-Indo-European *spewd- (“press, hurry”), whence English speed. Akin to Slovene speti (“to hasten”), Slovak dospieť (“grow mature”), Czech spět, spěch. Non-Slavic cognates include Sanskrit स्फायते (sphā́yatē, “to grow fat, increase”) and German sich sputen (“to hasten”).
  • с- (s-) +‎ петь (petʹ)
