мелькну́ть (3163)


Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *mer- (“to shimmer, glisten”). Cognates include Lithuanian mérkti (“to blink, twinkle”), Ancient Greek μέρα (méra, “morning”), Gothic 𐌼𐌰𐌿𐍂𐌲𐌹𐌽𐍃 (maurgins, “morning”), English morrow

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [mʲɪlʲkˈnutʲ]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Verb

мелькну́ть • (melʹknútʹ) pf (imperfective мелька́ть)

  1. to flash, to gleam
  2. to flit, to fly (past)
  3. to loom, to turn up
  4. to appear for a moment, to be glimpsed fleetingly
