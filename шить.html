шить -> to sew
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: b

Я шью
Ты шьёшь
Past: шил/шила



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *šiti, from Proto-Balto-Slavic *sjū́ˀtei, from Proto-Indo-European *syuh₁-.Cognate to Lithuanian siūti, English sew, Latin suō.



Definitions (English):
  • (transitive) to sew,
    • шить на маши́́нке ― šitʹ na mašínke ― to sew on a machine
  • (intransitive, only imperfective) to be occupied with sewing, to tailor.
    • Чем она́́ занима́ется? Она́ шьёт. ― Čem oná zanimájetsja? Ona šʹjót. ― What is she? She’s a dressmaker.
  • (colloquial) to order (e.g., a dress)
    • хочу́́ сшить себе́́ костю́м у Левинсона ― xočú sšitʹ sebé kostjúm u Levinsona ― I want to order a suit from Levinson’s
  • (only imperfective) to embroider,
    • шить зо́лотом ― šitʹ zólotom ― embroider in gold, шито бе́лыми ни́тками (saying) ― šito bélymi nítkami ― blatantly obvious, transparent to anyone (literally, “embroidered in white thread”)шить де́ло (colloquial) ― šitʹ délo ― to cook up charges (literally, “to embroider the case”)(он) не лы́ком шит (saying) ― (on) ne lýkom šit ― (he) was not born yesterday (literally, “(he) was not embroidered in bast”)
  • (figuratively) to nail planking,
    • шить плот ― šitʹ plot ― to make a raft
