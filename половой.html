полово́й (2548)


Etymology 1

пол (pol, “floor”) +‎ -овой (-ovój), from Old East Slavic полъ (polŭ, “floor”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [pəlɐˈvoj]

Adjective

полово́й • (polovój)

  1. (relational) floor, flooring (the bottom or lower part of a room)
    полова́я тря́пка ― polovája trjápka ― floorcloth

Noun

Полово́й с подно́сом и ча́йниками

полово́й • (polovój) m anim (genitive полово́го, nominative plural половы́е, genitive plural половы́х)

  1. (historical) waiter or servant in a Russian tavern

Etymology 2

пол (pol, “sex, gender”) +‎ -овой (-ovój), related to пол- (pol-, “half”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [pəlɐˈvoj]

Adjective

полово́й • (polovój)

  1. (relational, biology, sex) sex (as an action); sexual, genital
    полово́й акт ― polovój akt ― sex act, sexual intercourse
    полова́я связь ― polovája svjazʹ ― sexual relationship
    полово́й орган ― polovój órgan ― sexual organ
    полово́й отбо́р ― polovój otbór ― sexual selection

Etymology 3

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [pɐˈlovəj]

Noun

полово́й • (polóvoj) f inan

  1. instrumental singular of полова́ (polóva)
