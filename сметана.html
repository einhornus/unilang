смета́на (8856)


Etymology

From Proto-Slavic [Term?], cognate with Slovene smetana, Slovak smotana, Bulgarian смета́на (smetana). According to Max Vasmer">Vasmer, the Slavic words are unrelated to German Schmand, Schmant (“sour milk”). However, other scholars (Jacob Grimm">Jacob Grimm, Václav Machek) consider Schmand, Schmant to be a loanword from Slavic.[1] See Schmand for more.
