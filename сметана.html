смета́на -> sour cream
Noun, declension 1a (pattern де́вочка)

Plural: смета́ны
Genitive: смета́ны



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic [Term?], cognate with Slovene smetana, Slovak smotana, Bulgarian смета́на (smetana). According to Vasmer, the Slavic words are unrelated to German Schmand, Schmant (“sour milk”). However, other scholars (Jacob Grimm, Václav Machek) consider Schmand, Schmant to be a loanword from Slavic.Ώ] See Schmand for more.
