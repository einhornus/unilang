ко́бчик (29571)


Etymology

From коб(ец) (kób(ec), “small hawk, Pernis apivorus (European honey buzzard)”) +‎ -чик (-čik), from Proto-Slavic *kobьcь. Per Vasmer, probably cognate with English hawk, High German">Old High German habuh (“hawk”), Old Norse non">haukr (“hawk”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈkopt͡ɕɪk]
  • Homophone: ко́пчик (kópčik)

Noun

ко́бчик • (kóbčik) m anim (genitive ко́бчика, nominative plural ко́бчики, genitive plural ко́бчиков)

  1. red-footed falcon
