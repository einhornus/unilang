жрать -> to eat (very coll), gobble up
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: b/c

Я жру́
Ты жрёшь
Past: жра́л/жрала́



Etymology: From Old East Slavic жьрати (žĭrati), from Proto-Slavic *žьrati, from Proto-Indo-European *gʷerh₃- (“to devour”), whence English devour and voracity.



Definitions (English):
  • (low colloquial) to eat
    • (colloquial, of an animal) to gobble
      • (figuratively, low colloquial) to guzzle (alcohol)
        • (figuratively, colloquial) to guzzle (fuel or power)
          • (colloquial, of an insect) to sting, to bite
