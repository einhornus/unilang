елисе́йский (23900)


Etymology

From Элисий (Elísij, “Elysium”) +‎ -ский (-skij). Элисий (Elísij) is a less common form of Элизий (Elízij), alsó Элизиум (Elízium). The former term was borrowed directly from Ancient Greek Ἠλύσιον (Ēlúsion, “Elysian”), while the latter two terms came via Latin, in turn from the samé Greek word.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [(j)ɪlʲɪˈsʲejskʲɪj]

Adjective

елисе́йский • (jeliséjskij) (no comparative)

  1. Elysian, paradisiacal
    елисе́йские поля ― jeliséjskije poljá ― Champs-Élysées (famous avenue in Paris)

Related terms

  • Элисий (Elísij), Элизий (Elízij), Элизиум (Elízium)
