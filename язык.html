язы́к -> language, tongue
Noun, declension 2b (pattern язы́к)

Plural: языки́
Genitive: языка́



Etymology: From Old East Slavic ꙗзꙑкъ (jazykŭ), from Proto-Slavic *(j)ęzykъ, from Proto-Balto-Slavic *inźūˀkas, from Proto-Indo-European *dn̥ǵʰwéh₂s, whence English tongue and language.



Definitions (English):
  • (inanimate) tongue,
    • на ко́нчике языка́ ― na kónčike jazyká ― on the tip of (one's) tongue
  • (inanimate) language
    • (animate, military, slang) prisoner for interrogation, canary, informer
      • (inanimate) clapper (of a bell)
