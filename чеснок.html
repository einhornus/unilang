чесно́к (10823)


Etymology[edit]

From Old East Slavic чеснокъ (česnokŭ), *чеснъкъ (*česnŭkŭ), from Proto-Slavic *česnъkъ, from *česnъ (“garlic”) +‎ *-ъkъ.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [t͡ɕɪsˈnok]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun[edit]

чесно́к • (česnók) m inan (genitive чеснока́, nominative plural чесноки́, genitive plural чесноко́в, related adjective чесночный, diminutive чесночок)

  1. (usually uncountable, collectively) garlic, garlics
  2. caltrop
