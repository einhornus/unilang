каба́к (8538)


Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [kɐˈbak]

Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from Low German Kabacke (“hut, hovel”).

Noun[edit]

каба́к • (kabák) m inan (genitive кабака́, nominative plural кабаки́, genitive plural кабако́в, related adjective кабацкий, diminutive кабачо́к)

  1. tavern, pub, bar
  2. (colloquial, derogatory) watering hole, dive, joint
    гря́зный каба́к ― grjáznyj kabák ― crummy joint

Etymology 2[edit]

A Turkic borrowing, compare to Turkish kabak, Tatar каба́к (qabaq).

Noun[edit]

каба́к • (kabák) m inan (genitive кабака́, nominative plural кабаки́, genitive plural кабако́в)

  1. (Southern Russia) pumpkin
Related terms[edit]
  • кабачо́к m (kabačók)
