дитя -> child

Etymology: From Old East Slavic дѣтя (dětja), from Proto-Slavic *dětę. The и of the first syllable in the singular is from дити́на (ditína), nowadays written де́ти́на (detína) by analogy with the accented де́ти (déti), with regular distant assimilation ѣ…и́ > и…и́. An alternative formulation by Šakhmatov (ѣ > и before a soft consonant) explains these forms more directly.



Definitions (English):
  • (dated or poetic) child, baby, kid (also used ironically or in similes)
    • genitive singular of дитё (ditjó)
      • nominative plural of дитё (ditjó)
