госуда́рь (2576)


Etymology

From Old East Slavic госуда́рь (gosudarĭ), a later form of господарь (gospodarĭ).[1] The reshaping of the latter has been explained by the influence of the root суд- (sud-, “to judge”)[2] or by a relaxed pronunciation[3].

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ɡəsʊˈdarʲ]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

госуда́рь • (gosudárʹ) masculine gender">m anim (genitive госуда́ря, nominative plural госуда́ри, genitive plural госуда́рей, feminine госуда́рыня)

  1. sovereign
  2. Your Majesty
  3. sire
    ми́лостивый госуда́рь ― mílostivyj gosudárʹ ― Sir; Dear sir (in letters)

  • суда́рыня (sudárynja)
