клуб -> club
Noun, declension 2c (pattern го́род)

Plural: клубы́
Genitive: клу́ба



Etymologies:
  • From Old East Slavic клубъ (klubŭ), from Proto-Slavic *klǫbъ cognate with Ukrainian клуб (klub), Czech kloub, Polish kłąb.
  • Borrowed from German Klub, from English club.


Definitions (English):
  • cloud, puff (of smoke, etc.)
    • a dense swarm (of insects or other animals)
      • (dated) lump, ball
        • club (association of members)
          • clubhouse
