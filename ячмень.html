ячме́нь (21946)


Etymology

From Church Slavonic">Old Church Slavonic ѩчьнѣнъ (jęčĭněnŭ), from Proto-Slavic *(j)ęčьmy. Cognates include Ancient Greek ἀκοστή (akostḗ, “barley”), Latin acus (“needle”) and Old English ang">ahar (“corn-ear”)

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [(j)ɪt͡ɕˈmʲenʲ]
  • Audio

Noun

ячме́нь • (jačménʹ) masculine gender">m inan (genitive ячменя́, nominative plural ячмени́, genitive plural ячмене́й)

  1. stye (bacterial infection of the eyelash or eyelid)
  2. barley (annual temperate grasses of the species Hordeum vulgare)
  3. barley (the grain of annual temperate grasses of the species Hordeum vulgare, used in making beer and whisky and for soups, puddings, etc.)

Related terms
