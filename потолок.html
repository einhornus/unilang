потолк -> ceiling
Noun, declension 2b (pattern язк)

Plural: потолк
Genitive: потолк



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic [Term?], from *tьlo (“ground, bottom”), from Proto-Indo-European *telh₂-o- (“ground”), from *telh₂- (“to bear, carry”). Cognates include Sanskrit तल (tala, “surface, bottom”), Latin tellūs (“ground, land”)



Definitions (English):
  • ceiling,
    • плева́ть в потоло́к ― plevátʹ v potolók ― to sit around doing nothing, взять с потолка́ ― vzjatʹ s potolká ― to make up, to spin out of thin air
  • (figuratively) upper limit
    • (figuratively) limit in someone's development
      • (aviation) roof
        • masculine singular past indicative perfective of потолочь (potolóčʹ)
