сморчок (13478)


Pronunciation

IPA(key): [smɐrˈt͡ɕɵk]

Etymology 1

From Proto-Slavic *smъrčьkъ. Cognate with Serbo-Croatian смрчак, Slovene smȓček. Other languages have derivatives from Proto-Slavic *smъržь, e.g. Ukrainian сморж (smorž) (gen. sg. сморжа (smoržá)), also сморжій (smoržíj) (gen. sg. сморжія (smoržijá)), сморч (smorč) (gen. sg. сморча (smorčá)); Czech smrž; Slovak smrž; Polish language">Polish smarz, also smardz; Upper Sorbian language">Upper Sorbian smorž, also smorža; Lower Sorbian language">Lower Sorbian smarž. Which form is earlier is unclear, but Vasmer suggests a possible relationship with сморгать (smorgátʹ, “to blink”).

Noun

сморчок • (smorčók) masculine gender">m inan or masculine gender">m anim (genitive сморчка́, nominative plural сморчки́, genitive plural сморчко́в)

  1. (mycology) morel
  2. (colloquial, derogatory) wimp, nebbish (frail, nondescript man)
  3. (colloquial, derogatory) shortie, runt

Etymology 2

сморкать (smorkátʹ) +‎ -ок (-ók)

Noun

сморчок • (smorčók) masculine gender">m inan (genitive сморчка́, nominative plural сморчки́, genitive plural сморчко́в)

  1. (colloquial) act of blowing one's nose
