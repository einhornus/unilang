болеть -> to be ill, to hurt
Verb, imperfective, intransitive, stress pattern: a

Я болею
Ты болеешь
Past: болел/болела



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *bolěti.



Definitions (English):
  • to be sick, to be ill, to be ailing. Synonym:
    • хвора́ть (xvorátʹ)
  • to be anxious, to be apprehensive
    • to cheer, to root for.
      • Он боле́ет за Спарта́к. On boléjet za Sparták. He cheers for Spartak.
    • (intransitive) to hurt, to ache.
      • У меня́ бо́ли́т голова́́. U menjá bolít golová. I have a headache.(literally, “There is pain in my head.”) У меня́ бо́ли́т зуб. U menjá bolít zub. I have a toothache.(literally, “There is pain in my tooth.”) У меня́ бо́ли́т го́рло. U menjá bolít górlo. I have a sore throat.(literally, “There is pain in my throat.”) У меня́ боля́т глаза́. U menjá bolját glazá. My eyes hurt.(literally, “There is pain in my eyes.”) У меня́ боля́т но́ги. U menjá bolját nógi. My legs ache.(literally, “There is pain in my legs.”)
