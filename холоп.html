холо́п (17069)


Etymology

From Proto-Slavic *xolpъ. Cognate with Church Slavonic">Old Church Slavonic хлапъ (xlapŭ) with the same meaning or Polish language">Polish chłop (“peasant”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [xɐˈlop]

Noun

холо́п • (xolóp) masculine gender">m anim (genitive холо́па, nominative plural холо́пы or холопья or холопи*, genitive plural холо́пов or холопьев or холопей*) (* Both irregular plurals are archaic.)

  1. (historical) serf, bondman, kholop

Related terms

  • хло́пец (xlópec, “boy, lad”) (via Ukrainian or Polish)
