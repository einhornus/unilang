кипе́ть -> to boil
Verb, imperfective, intransitive, stress pattern: b

Я киплю́
Ты кипи́шь
Past: кипе́л/кипе́ла



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *kypě̀ti, from Proto-Indo-European *kwep-, whence English cupidity, covet and hope.



Definitions (English):
  • to boil, to seethe,
    • кипе́ть ключо́м ― kipétʹ ključóm ― to boil over
  • to boil, to rage, to seethe, to burn (in terms of emotions)
    • кипе́ть зло́бой ― kipétʹ zlóboj ― to boil with anger
  • to be in full swing (in terms of action)
    • Рабо́та кипи́т. ― Rabóta kipít. ― The work is in full swing.
