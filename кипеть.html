ки́петь -> to boil, be boiling
Verb, imperfective, intransitive, stress pattern: b

Я киплю
Ты ки́пишь
Past: ки́пел/ки́пела



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *kypě̀ti, from Proto-Indo-European *kwep-, whence English cupidity, covet and hope.



Definitions (English):
  • to boil, to seethe,
    • ки́пе́ть ключо́м ― kipétʹ ključóm ― to boil over
  • to boil, to rage, to seethe, to burn (in terms of emotions)
    • ки́пе́ть зло́бой ― kipétʹ zlóboj ― to boil with anger
  • to be in full swing (in terms of action)
    • Рабо́та́ кипи́́т. ― Rabóta kipít. ― The work is in full swing.
