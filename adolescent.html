adolescent (4330)


Etymology

First attested 1482, from French adolescent, from Latin adolescentem, accusative form of adolescens, present participle of adolēscere (“to become adult, grow up”), from ad- (“to”) + alēscere (“to grow or become nourished”). The adjective first appeared in 1785.

Pronunciation

  • EnPR: ăd'əlĕsʹənt, IPA(key): /ˌædəˈlɛsənt/
    • Audio (UK)
      		(file)

Adjective

adolescent (comparative more adolescent, superlative most adolescent)

  1. Of, relating to, or at the age of adolescence; at the stage betweeń being a child and an adult
    • 1785, William Cowper, Tirocinium
      Schools, unless discipline were doubly strong, / Detain their adolescent charge too long.

Related terms

  • adolescence
  • adult

Noun

adolescent (plural adolescents)

  1. A person who is in adolescence; someone who has reached puberty but is not yet an adult.
