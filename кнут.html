кнут -> whip, knout
Noun, declension 2b (pattern язы́к)

Plural: кнуты́
Genitive: кнута́



Etymology: From Old East Slavic кнутъ (knutŭ), from Old Norse knútr (“knot”). The original meaning was “knotty whip”.



Definitions (English):
  • whip, knout, scourge
