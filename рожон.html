рожо́н (16973)


Etymology

From Proto-Slavic *oržьnь.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [rɐˈʐon]

Noun

рожо́н • (rožón) m inan (genitive рожна́, nominative plural рожны́, genitive plural рожно́в)

  1. (dated) pointed stake (placed at an angle in a defensive fortification)
  2. (colloquial) trouble
    лезть на рожо́н ― leztʹ na rožón ― to ask for trouble, act recklessly
