клева́ть -> to peck
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: b

Я клюю́
Ты клюёшь
Past: клева́л/клева́ла



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *kľьvati. Compare Bulgarian кълва (kǎlvá), Belarusian клюваць (kljuvácʹ), Old Church Slavonic клюıѫ (kljuıǫ), Slovene kljuváti, Polish kluć, Ukrainian клювати (kljuváty).



Definitions (English):
  • to peck, to pick (for birds)
    • клева́ть но́сом ― klevátʹ nósom ― to nod off to sleep, to be drowsy
  • to bite (in terms of biting a baited hook or other lure)
