виски (4733)


Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from English whiskey, whisky.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ˈvʲiskʲɪ]

Noun[edit]

виски • (víski) n inan or m inan (indeclinable)

  1. whiskey / whisky
Usage notes[edit]

The gender was originally feminine (in a translation of Jack London stories, 1915), then masculine (e.g. "Без же́нщин" by Alexander Vertinsky), and today it has become neuter. The masculine variant is typically considered archaic, and the feminine one is completely forgotten.
