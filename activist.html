activist (0)


Etymology

active +‎ -ist

Pronunciation

  • (American English">US) IPA(key): /ˈæk.tɪ.vɪst/

Noun

activist (plural activists)

  1. One who is politically active in the role of a citizen; especially, one who campaigns for change.
    • 2013 August 10, “external text" href="http://www.economist.com/news/leaders/21583277-worlds-biggest-polluter-going-green-it-needs-speed-up-transition-can-china">Can China clean up fast enough?”, in <cite>The Economist">The Economist, volume 408, number 8848:
      It has jailed environmental activists and is planning to limit the power of judicial oversight by handing a state-approved body a monopoly over bringing environmental lawsuits.
  2. One who is conspicuously active in carrying out any occupational or professional functions.

Synonyms

  • mover and shaker

Related terms
