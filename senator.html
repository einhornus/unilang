senator (2168)


Etymology

From senātus (“senate”) +‎ -tor, originally from senex (“old”).

Pronunciation

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /seˈnaː.tor/, [sɛˈnaː.t̪ɔr]
  • (Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /seˈna.tor/, [sɛˈnaː.t̪ɔr]

Noun

senātor m (genitive senātōris); third declension

  1. senator, member of the Roman Senate

Related terms

  • senātus
  • seneciō
  • senectus
  • seneō
  • senex
