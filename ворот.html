во́рот (778)


Etymology 1

From Old East Slavic воротъ (vorotŭ), from Proto-Slavic *vortъ. Cognate with Church Slavonic">Old Church Slavonic вратъ (vratŭ). Related to верте́ть (vertétʹ).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈvorət]
  • Rhymes: -ot

Noun

во́рот • (vórot) m inan (genitive во́рота, nominative plural во́роты, genitive plural во́ротов)

  1. collar (of a shirt)
  2. winch, windlass, wheel and axle
Synonyms
  • воротник (vorotník)
Related terms
  • воротник (vorotník), во́рота (voróta), поворо́т (povorót)
  • отвора́чиваться (otvoráčivatʹsja)
