сорока -> magpie
Noun, declension 1a (pattern де́вочка)

Plural: соро́ки
Genitive: соро́ки



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *s(v)orka.



Definitions (English):
  • European magpie ( Pica pica) ( Russians associate the magpie with unpleasant squawking, complaining, and especially thievery)1886,

Artyom took a timid sideway glance at his visitor and twitched his beard as a magpie twitches her tail.1893, Антон Павлович Чехов ( Anton Pavlovich Chekhov), “Рассказ неизве́стного челове́ка”, in Ру́сская мысль; translated as “An Anonymous Story”, in Constance Garnett, transl., The Lady with the Dog and Other Stories, London: Chatto & Windus, 1917, section 6:— Тепе́рь я понима́ю, отчего́ я так ча́сто теря́ю свои́ платки́ и перча́тки.
