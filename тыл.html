тыл -> rear (military)
Noun, declension 2c (pattern го́род)

Plural: тылы́
Genitive: ты́ла



Etymologies:
  • From Old East Slavic тꙑлъ (tylŭ), from Proto-Slavic *tylъ (“back of the head, back”).
  • From Proto-Uralic *tule.
  • From Proto-Turkic *dɨl (“tongue”). Cognate with Turkish dil.


Definitions (English):
  • back, rear,
    • с ты́ла ― s týla ― at the rear/from the rear/in the rear, подходи́ть/подойти́ с ты́ла/ты́лу ― podxodítʹ/podojtí s týla/týlu ― to approach from the back
  • (military) rear (the part of an army or fleet which comes last)
    • Тыл Вооружëнных Си́л Росси́йской Федера́ции ― Tyl Vooružënnyx Sil Rossíjskoj Federácii ― Rear of the Armed Forces of the Russian Federation
  • (military) home front,
    • наро́дная борьба́ в ты́лу ― naródnaja borʹbá v tylú ― popular struggle in the homefront, служи́ть/послужи́ть в ты́лу ― služítʹ/poslužítʹ v tylú ― to serve in the homefront
  • fire
    • Yakut
      • tongue, language
