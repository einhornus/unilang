набалда́шник (26130)


Etymology[edit]

From *набалдачник (*nabaldáčnik), from на- (na-) +‎ балдак (baldák, “large glass, cone, head”) +‎ -ник (-nik), from Tatar балдак (baldaq, “hilt of a saber, knob on a saber handle, smooth ring without a stone”), or from Ottoman Turkish بالداق‎ (baldak, “(same meaning)”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [nəbɐlˈdaʂnʲɪk]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun[edit]

набалда́шник • (nabaldášnik) m inan (genitive набалда́шника, nominative plural набалда́шники, genitive plural набалда́шников)

  1. knob (on the end of a cane or stick)

Related terms[edit]

  • балдак (baldák)
