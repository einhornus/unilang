скита́ться -> to wander, roam
Verb, imperfective, intransitive, stress pattern: a

Я скита́юсь
Ты скита́ешься
Past: скита́лся/скита́лась



Etymology: From Old East Slavic скытати ся (skytati sja), cognate with Serbian Church Slavonic скытати сѧ (skytati sę), Ukrainian скитатися (skytátysja), Bulgarian скитам се (skítam se), Serbo-Croatian скитати се, Slovene skítati sе. More distantly cognate with Lithuanian kutėti (“to shake”), Old Norse skundа (“to hurry”), Old Saxon scuddian (“to shake, to push”), Old High German scutten (“to shake, to push”).



Definitions (English):
  • to wander, to stray,
    • скита́ться по белу све́ту ― skitatʹsja po bélu svétu ― to knock about the world
