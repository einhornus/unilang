крестьянин -> peasant
Noun, declension 2a (pattern мальчик)

Plural: крестьяне
Genitive: крестьянина



Etymology: From Old East Slavic крьстьꙗнинъ (krĭstĭjaninŭ, “Christian human, person tax-paying villager”), крьстьꙗнъ (krĭstĭjanŭ, “Christian peasant, tiller tax-paying villager”), from Latin christiānus (“Christian”). For semantic shift to "peasant", compare English cretin, also from Latin christiānus, through French.



Definitions (English):
  • farmer
    • (historical) peasant
