моря́к -> sailor
Noun, declension 2b (pattern язы́к)

Plural: моряки́
Genitive: моряка́



Etymology: From мо́ре (móre, “sea”) +‎ -як (-jak), from Proto-Slavic *moře, from Proto-Indo-European *móri, whence English marine and maritime.



Definitions (English):
  • (nautical) seaman, sailor
    • (meteorology) sea breeze (a strong, biting wind that blows in from the sea around the mouths of rivers)
