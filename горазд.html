гора́зд (22791)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *gorazdъ. Per Vasmer, an ancient borrowing from Gothic, from Proto-Germanic *ga- + *razdō (“voice, sound”); originally "speaking intelligently".

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ɡɐˈrast]

Adjective[edit]

гора́зд • (gorázd)

  1. (colloquial) skilful; capable (of doing something)
    гора́зд на вы́думки ― gorázd na výdumki ― good with inventions/contrivance
