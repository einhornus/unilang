ду́ля (19451)


Etymology

From earlier гдуля (gdúlja) and гдуня (gdúnja), from Proto-Slavic *kъduňa, from Latin cydōnia, from Ancient Greek Κυδωνία (Kudōnía, “quince-tree”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈdulʲə]
  • Audio

Noun

ду́ля • (dúlja) f inan (genitive ду́ли, nominative plural ду́ли, genitive plural ду́ль)

  1. (dialectal) small pear
  2. sign">fig sign (a rude gesture in which the hand makes a fist and the thumb is stuck betweeń the index and middle fingers); indicates "nothing for you", "nuts to you"
