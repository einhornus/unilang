гу́ща -> dregs, thicket
Noun, declension 1a (pattern де́вочка)

Plural: гу́щи
Genitive: гу́щи



Etymology: From Old East Slavic гу́ща (gušča), from Proto-Slavic *gǫšča, from *gǫstъ (“dense”) + *-ja.



Definitions (English):
  • sediment, (coffee) grounds, (beer) lees, soup the body,
    • гада́ть на кофейной гу́ще ― gadátʹ na koféjnoj gúšče ― to read the leaves, to make wild guesses (lit: tell fortunes by coffee grounds)
  • thicket,
    • в гу́ще лесаv gúšče lesáin the thick of the forest
  • thick, centre, middle,
    • в са́мую гу́щу ма́сс, v sámuju gúšču massin the very midst of the masses, в самой гу́ще собы́тий, v samoj gúšče sobýtijin the thick of things
