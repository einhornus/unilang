ра́ковина -> (sea)shell, sink, wash-basin
Noun, declension 1a (pattern де́вочка)

Plural: ра́ковины
Genitive: ра́ковины



Etymology: From Old East Slavic ра́ковина (rakovina, “mother of pearl”). Per Vasmer, from Proto-Slavic *orky (“shell”) (genitive *orkъve) + *-ina, from Proto-Germanic *arkō (“chest, coffer”), from Latin arca (“chest, coffer, coffin”). Cognate with рака (ráka, “shrine of a saint, originally coffin”), from the same Latin source, and with Slovene rákəv (“coffin, crypt”), Czech rakev (“coffin”), rakvice (“shell”), Slovak rakev (“box”), Polabian rakåí (“box”).



Definitions (English):
  • shell (hard calcareous external covering of mollusks)
    • (anatomy) pinna, auricle, helix (the external ear)
      • sink, washbowl, basin, bowl
        • vesicle
          • bandstand
            • (metallurgy) blister, cavity, bubble, flaw, blowhole
