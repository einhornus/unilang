Noun[edit]

ба́рин • (bárin) m anim (genitive ба́рина, nominative plural ба́ре, genitive plural бар, feminine ба́рыня)

  1. (historical) boyar, barin, nobleman, gentleman, landlord (a rank of aristocracy in Russia)
    хозя́ин — ба́рин (idiom) ― xozjáin — bárin (idiom) ― it's up to you, your choice, you're the boss.
  2. (archaic) a form of address to noble people, sir, master
  3. a person who doesn't want to work
    сиде́ть ба́рином; сиде́ть как ба́рин ― sidétʹ bárinom; sidétʹ kak bárin ― to sit around like royalty

Synonyms[edit]

  • боя́рин (bojárin) (boyar; an older term)
  • господи́н (gospodín), госуда́рь (gosudárʹ), су́дарь (súdarʹ) (archaic forms of address)
  • лорд (lord) (noble man)

Related terms[edit]

  • ба́рский (bárskij)
