дре́во (14884)


Etymology

Borrowed from Church Slavonic">Old Church Slavonic дрѣво (drěvo). Doublet of дерево (dérevo), the inherited Old East Slavic form.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈdrʲevə]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

дре́во • (drévo) n inan (genitive дре́ва, nominative plural древеса́, genitive plural древе́с)

  1. (archaic or literary) tree

Noun

дре́во • (drévo) n inan (genitive дре́ва, nominative plural дре́ва, genitive plural дре́в)

  1. tree (in the expressions родословное дре́во (rodoslóvnoje drévo, “family tree”), генеалоги́ческое дре́во (genealogíčeskoje drévo, “family tree”), дре́во жи́зни (drévo žízni, “tree of life”))

Related terms

  • дре́вко n (drevkó)
  • древе́сный (drevésnyj)
