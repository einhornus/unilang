бесноватый (27630)


Etymology

16th-century construction from obsolete бесный (bésnyj) +‎ -ватый (-vátyj), from Old East Slavic бѣсьныи (běsĭnyi), from Proto-Slavic *běsьnъ; see Russian бес (bes). Ukrainian біснуватий (bisnuvatyj) is a borrowing from Russian.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [bʲɪsnɐˈvatɨj]

Adjective

бесноватый • (besnovátyj)

  1. (dated) possessed by demons
  2. (colloquial) furious, frantic, frenzied, raging

Noun

бесноватый • (besnovátyj) m anim (genitive беснова́того, nominative plural беснова́тые, genitive plural беснова́тых)

  1. possessed person, person possessed by demons

Related terms

  • бес (bes)
