игра -> game
Noun, declension 1d (pattern стра́на)

Plural: игры
Genitive: игры



Etymology: Inherited from Old East Slavic игра́ (igra), from Proto-Slavic *jьgra



Definitions (English):
  • game,
    • игра́́ в мяч ― igrá v mjač ― ball game
  • a sport which is played (also
    • спорти́вная игра́)
  • (action): play in the following senses:Activity for amusement only, especially among the young. Similar activity, in young animals, as they explore their environment and learn new skills.(ethology) "Repeated, incompletely functional behavior differing from more serious versions ..., and initiated voluntarily when ... in a low-stress setting."An individual's performance in a sport or game.
    • acting, performance,
      • игра́́ актёра ― igrá aktjóra ― an actor’s performance
    • role-playing (also
      • ролевая игра́)
    • playing (a musical instrument)
      • игра́́ на гита́ре ― igrá na gitáre ― playing the guitar
