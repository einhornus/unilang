пра́вый (7367)


Etymology

From Proto-Slavic *pravъ, from Proto-Indo-European *ProHwo- (“right judge, master”), ultimately from *per-. Compare правда (právda). Cognate with Latin prōvincia (“territory, dominion, office, duty, province”), Proto-Germanic *frawjô (“lord”).

Pronunciation

  • audio
    		(file)
  • IPA(key): [ˈpravɨj]

Adjective

пра́вый • (právyj) (comparative (по)правее or (по)правей, superlative са́мый пра́вый)

  1. right, right-hand
  2. (nautical) starboard
  3. right-wing
  4. right, correct
    Они пра́вы во всём. ― Oní právy vo vsjóm. ― They are right in everything.
  5. righteous
    пра́вое дело ― právoje délo ― just cause

Usage notes

  • In the meaning "correct", the adjective is more commonly used in the short forms. In the meanings "on the right side" and "right-wing", the short forms are rare.

Noun

пра́вый • (právyj) masculine gender">m anim (genitive пра́вого, nominative plural пра́вые, genitive plural пра́вых, feminine пра́вая)

  1. rightist
