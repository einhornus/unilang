пере́ть -> to shove, barge, push (coll)
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: b

Я пру́
Ты прёшь
Past: пёр/пёрла



Etymology: Inherited from Proto-Slavic *perti, from Proto-Indo-European *prey, whence English fare and ferry as well as German fahren.



Definitions (English):
  • (colloquial, derogatory) to move, to trudge, to drag the feet. Synonym:
    • передвигаться (peredvigátʹsja)
  • (colloquial) to travel (on a burdensome journey) Synonym:
    • передвигаться (peredvigátʹsja)
  • (colloquial) to carry (a burden) Synonym:
    • тащи́ть (taščítʹ)
  • (colloquial) to steal, to pinch. Perfectives:
    • спереть (sperétʹ), упереть (uperétʹ) Synonym: тащи́ть (taščítʹ)
  • (colloquial, derogatory) to give off an unpleasant odour, to stink.
    • От тебя́ прёт чесноком. ― Ot tebjá prjót česnokóm. ― You reek of garlic.
  • (slang, impersonal) to enjoy, to revel.
    • Меня прёт от э́того музла ― Menjá prjót ot étovo muzlá ― This music makes me revel. Synonym: тащи́ть (taščítʹ)
