сосло́вие (10990)


Etymology

From Old Church Slavonic съсловие (sŭslovie), itself a calque of Ancient Greek σύγκλητος (súnklētos) / σύλλογος (súllogos). Morphologically, from со- (so-) +‎ сло́во (slóvo) +‎ -ие (-ije).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [sɐsˈlovʲɪje]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

сосло́вие • (soslóvije) n inan (genitive сосло́вия, nominative plural сосло́вия, genitive plural сосло́вий, related adjective сосло́вный)

  1. (historical) social estate
  2. social class, social rank (with strong connotations of cultural casté)
