дудка -> pipe, fife
Noun, declension 1a (pattern девочка)

Plural: дудки
Genitive: дудки



Etymology: From дуда́ (dudá, “fife, pipe”) +‎ -ка (-ka). Miklosich and Berneker considered this Slavic word to be borrowed from Turkic, but Vasmer and Brückner believe that the close sound rendition of these onomatopoetic words is a "mere chance". Cognate with Lithuanian daudýtė (“panpipe”).



Definitions (English):
  • fife, pipe (small shrill pipe)
    • пляса́ть под чью́́-либо ду́дку (idiomatic) ― pljasátʹ pod čʹjú-libo dúdku ― to dance to somebody's tune / piping
