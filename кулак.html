кулк -> fist, kulak (rich peasant)
Noun, declension 2b (pattern язк)

Plural: кула́к
Genitive: кула́к



Etymology: Probably from Turkic, with the same meaning, from *kul (“hand”). Compare Bashkir ҡул (qul, “arm, hand”), Tatar кул (qul, “arm, hand”) and Turkish kol (“arm”). The sense of “wealthy farmer” is from the expression держа́ть в кулаке́ (deržátʹ v kulaké, “to keep in dependence”) or developed figuratively as “fist” -> “tightfisted person”.
