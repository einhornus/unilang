мстер -> master
Noun, declension 2c (pattern грод)

Plural: ма́стер
Genitive: мстера



Etymology: From Old East Slavic мастеръ (masterŭ, “Master of the Order”), from Old French maistre, from Latin magister (“master, chief, head”). Doublet of мэтр (mɛtr).



Definitions (English):
  • master, craftsman, expert
    • foreman
      • repairman
        • (computing) wizard (program or script used to simplify complex operations)
