шку́ра -> skin, hide
Noun, declension 1a (pattern де́вочка)

Plural: шку́ры
Genitive: шку́ры



Etymology: Likely a borrowing from Polish skóra, from Proto-Slavic *skora (“bast, skin”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ker- (“to cut”), whence English carnage, cortex, carrion, corium, scurf and excoriate. Doublet of скора́ (skorá), the inherited East Slavic form.



Definitions (English):
  • (of animals) skin, pelt, coat
    • (derogatory) bastard, scum
      • (derogatory) whore
