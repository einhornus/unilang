помочь -> to help (s.o.)
Verb, perfective, intransitive, stress pattern: c/b

Я помо́гу
Ты поможешь
Past: помог/помогла



Etymologies:
  • по- (po-) +‎ мочь (močʹ), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *megʰ-, whence English might.
  • Inherited from Proto-Slavic *pomoťь. Doublet of по́мощь (pómoščʹ), a borrowing from Old Church Slavonic.


Definitions (English):
  • to help (+ dative)
    • Я хочу́́ тебе́́ помо́чь. ― Ja xočú tebé pomóčʹ. ― I want to help you.
  • to aid, to assist (+ dative)
    • to avail (+ dative)
      • (usually plural) belt, tape, or restraint to hold, support, or suspend something or someone (also figurative)
        • (usually plural) suspenders ( US), braces ( Britain)
          • (historical) (usually plural) leading-string
            • (obsolete or regional) a gathering of friends and neighbors to help in some project (such as a quilting bee, a barn-raising bee)
              • (obsolete or regional) synonymous with по́мощь (pómoščʹ): help, assistance, aid, support
