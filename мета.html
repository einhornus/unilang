ме́та (25968)


Etymology 1[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • ме́та: IPA(key): [ˈmʲetə]

Noun[edit]

ме́та • (méta) f inan (genitive ме́ты, nominative plural ме́ты, genitive plural ме́т)

  1. (dated) mark, label, sign
  2. (dated) distinguishing characteristic (what distinguishes two otherwise identical or similar things)
  3. (economics) profit sharing contract (contract where the participants equally share profits and losses)
Related terms[edit]
  • метка (métka)
  • поме́та (pométa), поме́тка (pométka)
  • ме́тить impf (métitʹ), поме́тить pf (pométitʹ)

Etymology 2[edit]

Borrowed from Latin mēta.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • ме́та: IPA(key): [ˈmʲetə]
  • ме́та: IPA(key): [mʲɪˈta]

Noun[edit]

ме́та or ме́та • (méta or metá) f inan (genitive ме́ты or ме́ты, nominative plural ме́ты, genitive plural ме́т)

  1. (dated) goal, target
  2. (dated, figuratively) object of desire
