хлопота́ть (7190)


Etymology

  • Old Church Slavonic клопотъ (klopotŭ) +‎ -ать (-atʹ). Originally as клопотать (klopotátʹ). Cognates include Bulgarian клопотя (klopotjá, “to bring to tears”), Czech klороtаti (“to knock”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [xləpɐˈtatʲ]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Verb

хлопота́ть • (xlopotátʹ) impf (perfective похлопота́ть or захлопотать)

  1. to bustle about, to diligently do things
  2. to hustle, to fuss
  3. to try to achieve
    не хлопочи́те ― ne xlopočíte ― don't bother
  4. to try to help, to intercede (for), to plead (for) (usually with the authorities)

Related terms

  • хло́поты f or pl (xlópoty)
  • хлопотли́вый (xlopotlívyj), хло́потный (xlópotnyj)
  • хлопотун m (xlopotún), хлопотунья f (xlopotúnʹja)
