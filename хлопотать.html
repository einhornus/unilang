хлопотть -> to bustle about, make efforts
Verb, imperfective, intransitive, stress pattern: c

Я хлопоч
Ты хлопчешь
Past: хлопотл/хлопотла



Etymology: Old Church Slavonic клопотъ (klopotŭ) +‎ -ать (-atʹ). Originally as клопотать (klopotátʹ). Cognates include Bulgarian клопотя (klopotjá, “to bring to tears”), Czech klороtаti (“to knock”).



Definitions (English):
  • to bustle about, to diligently do things
    • to hustle, to fuss
      • to try to achieve,
        • не хлопочи́те ― ne xlopočíte ― don't bother
      • to try to help, to intercede (for), to plead (for) (usually with the authorities)
