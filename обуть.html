обу́ть -> to put shoes on s.o., provide with shoes
Verb, perfective, transitive, stress pattern: a

Я обу́ю
Ты обу́ешь
Past: обу́л/обу́ла



Etymology: об- (ob-) +‎ -уть (-utʹ). From Proto-Slavic *obuti.



Definitions (English):
  • to put shoes on somebody's feet, to help somebody put on his/her shoes,
    • обу́ть ноги ― obútʹ nógi ― to put on shoes
  • to provide with shoes
    • (slang) to trick, to take in, to snooker
