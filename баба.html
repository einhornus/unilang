баба -> peasant woman, (coll) old woman, woman
Noun, declension 1a (pattern девочка)

Plural: бабы
Genitive: бабы



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *baba.



Definitions (English):
  • (childish) grandma, granny (short for
    • ба́бушка (bábuška)) Ма́ма, а где ба́ба? ― Máma, a gde bába? ― Mom, where’s grandma?
  • (informal or archaic) old woman (short for
    • ба́бушка (bábuška)) Жи́ли-бы́ли дед да ба́ба. ― Žíli-býli ded da bába. ― There once lived an old man and his old woman.
  • (historical) (any) woman countrywoman
    • (colloquial, slang, derogatory) woman, female, broad countrywoman.
      • Кто э́та́ ба́ба? ― Kto éta bába? ― Who's that broad? Все ба́бы — ду́ры все мужики́́ — сво́лочи (contemporary Russian saying) Vse báby — dúry vse mužikí — svóloči"Every woman is stupid, every man is a bastard"
    • (obsolete) peasant’s wife, peasant woman
      • (derogatory) milksop, old woman (weak man)
        • (technical) ram (of a pile driver)
          • (technical) drop weight
            • (cooking) baba (tall, cylindrical cake), bundt cake, Gugelhupf,
              • ро́мовая ба́ба ― rómovaja bába ― rum baba, rum cake
            • in expressions,
              • сне́жная ба́ба ― snéžnaja bába ― snowman, ка́менная ба́ба ― kámennaja bába ― primitive stone statue
