дёргать -> to tug
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: a

Я дёргаю
Ты дёргаешь
Past: дёргал/дёргала



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *dьrgati (“to pull, to comb (flax)?”).



Definitions (English):
  • to pull, to tug,
    • дёргать кого́́-либо за рука́́в ― djórgatʹ kovó-libo za rukáv ― to pull smb's sleeve tug at smb's sleeve
  • to pull out,
    • дёргать зуб ― djórgatʹ zub ― to pull out a tooth
  • (impersonal, colloquial) to twitch, to throb, to twinge,
    • его́́ всего́́ дёргает ― jevó vsevó djórgajet ― he is twitching all over, у меня́ дёргает па́лец ― u menjá djórgajet pálec ― my finger throbs I have twinges [shooting pains] in my finger
  • to move sharply, to jerk
    • (colloquial) to disturb,
      • дёргать подчинённых ― djórgatʹ podčinjónnyx ― to harass one's subordinates
