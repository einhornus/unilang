па́кость -> dirty trick, filth
Noun, declension 3a (pattern жизнь)

Plural: па́кости
Genitive: па́кости



Etymology: Inherited from Old East Slavic па́кость (pakostĭ), from Proto-Slavic *pakostь (“vicissitude”). Compare Old Church Slavonic па́кость (pakostĭ, “madness, tyranny”), Ukrainian пакість (pákistʹ) (gen. па́кости (pákosty)), Belarusian пакосць (pákoscʹ), Bulgarian пакост (pákost, “harm”), Serbo-Croatian па̏ко̄ст (“malice”), Slovene pákost (“vicissitude”) (tonal orthography), Czech раkоsť (“filth”), Polish раkоść, Upper Sorbian раkоsć, Lower Sorbian раkоsć. For the original meaning, compare Old Church Slavonic опако (opako, “backwards”), пакы (paky, “meanwhile”).
