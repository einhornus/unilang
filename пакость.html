па́кость (11296)


Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Old East Slavic па́кость (pakostĭ), from Proto-Slavic *pakostь (“vicissitude”). Compare Old Church Slavonic па́кость (pakostĭ, “madness, tyranny”), Ukrainian пакість (pákistʹ) (gen. па́кости (pákosty)), Belarusian пакосць (pákoscʹ), Bulgarian пакост (pákost, “harm”), Serbo-Croatian па̏ко̄ст (“malice”), Slovene pákost (“vicissitude”) (tonal orthography), Czech раkоsť (“filth”), Polish раkоść, Upper Sorbian раkоsć, Lower Sorbian раkоsć. For the original meaning, compare Old Church Slavonic опако (opako, “backwards”), пакы (paky, “meanwhile”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ˈpakəsʲtʲ]

Noun[edit]

па́кость • (pákostʹ) f inan (genitive па́кости, nominative plural па́кости, genitive plural па́костей)

  2. dirty/mean trick

  3. filthy language
