нет -> no, (+g) there is no

Etymology: From Old East Slavic нѣту́ (nětu), Proto-Slavic *nětu, from *ne (“not”) + *e, short form of *estь (“is”) + *tu (“there, here”).



Definitions (English):
  • no,
    • ещё нет ― ješčó net ― not yet
  • there is no, there are no (+ genitive)
    • Здесь нет доро́г. Zdesʹ net doróg. There are no roads here. У меня нет кни́г. U menjá net knig. I have no books. (literally, “There are no books with me.”) Его́ нет. Jevó net. He’s not here. (literally, “There is no him [here].”)
