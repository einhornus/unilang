я́ма -> pit, hole
Noun, declension 1a (pattern де́вочка)

Plural: я́мы
Genitive: я́мы



Etymologies:
  • Inherited from Old East Slavic я́ма (jama), from Proto-Slavic *ama
  • Borrowed from Sanskrit यम (yama).


Definitions (English):
  • pit, hole (in the ground)
    • dungeon
      • yama
        • genitive singular of я́м (jam)
