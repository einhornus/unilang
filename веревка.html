веревка (1993)


Etymology

From Old East Slavic вьрвь (vĭrvĭ), from Proto-Slavic *vьrvь (deverbal of *verti), from Proto-Indo-European *wer- (“to tie, string; rope”) which is ultimately cognate with *wer- (“to turn, bend”) whence the two meanings of the verb. Compare Ukrainian вірьовка (virʹovka), Belarusian вяроўка (vjaróŭka), Church Slavonic">Old Church Slavonic връвь (vrŭvĭ). Non-Slavic cognates include Lithuanian virvė, Latvian virve, Latgalian vērve (“rope, cord, line”), Ancient Greek ἐρύω (erúō, “I drag, pull”) and Sanskrit वरत्रा (varatrā).
