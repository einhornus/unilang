кишка́ (4177)


Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Slavic *kyšьka.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [kʲɪʂˈka]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun[edit]

кишка́ • (kišká) f inan (genitive кишки́, nominative plural кишки́, genitive plural кишо́к)

  1. (anatomy) gut, intestine, bowel
    то́нкая кишка́ ― tónkaja kišká ― small intestine
    то́лстая кишка́ ― tólstaja kišká ― large intestine
    двенадцатиперстная кишка́ ― dvenadcatipérstnaja kišká ― duodenum
    пряма́я кишка́ ― prjamája kišká ― rectum
    слепа́я кишка́ ― slepája kišká ― blind gut, caecum
    У него́ кишка́ тонка́! ― U nevó kišká tonká! ― he isn't up to it!
  2. (figuratively, colloquial, plural only) giblets, pluck
  3. (figuratively, colloquial, plural only) insides, contents, spares
  4. (figuratively, colloquial) hose (for watering)
  5. (figuratively, colloquial, rare) tunnel, tunnel-like room

Related terms[edit]

  • кише́чный (kišéčnyj)
  • кише́чник (kišéčnik)
