кишка -> gut, intestine
Noun, declension 1b
Plural: кишки
Genitive: кишки



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *kyšьka.



Definitions (English):
  • (anatomy) gut, intestine, bowel,
    • то́нкая кишка́́ ― tónkaja kišká ― small intestine, то́лстая кишка́́ ― tólstaja kišká ― large intestine, двенадцатипе́рстная кишка́́ ― dvenadcatipérstnaja kišká ― duodenum, пряма́́я кишка́́ ― prjamája kišká ― rectum, слепа́́я кишка́́ ― slepája kišká ― blind gut, caecum. У него́́ кишка́́ тонка́́! ― U nevó kišká tonká! ― he isn't up to it!
  • (figuratively, colloquial, plural only) giblets, pluck
    • (figuratively, colloquial, plural only) insides, contents, spares
      • (figuratively, colloquial) hose (for watering)
        • (figuratively, colloquial, rare) tunnel, tunnel-like room
