дёрнуть -> to tug
Verb, perfective, transitive, stress pattern: a

Я дёрну
Ты дёрнешь
Past: дёрнул/дёрнула



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *dьrnǫti.



Definitions (English):
  • to pull, to tug,
    • дёрнуть кого́-либо за рука́в ― djórnutʹ kovó-libo za rukáv ― to pull smb's sleeve tug at smb's sleeve, и дёрнуло меня! ― i djórnulo menjá! ― what (on earth) possessed me! (literally, “and it pulled me!”)чёрт меня дёрнул! ― čórt menjá djórnul! ― what (on earth) possessed me! (literally, “the devil pulled me!”)
  • to pull out,
    • дёрнуть зуб ― djórnutʹ zub ― to pull out a tooth
  • (impersonal, colloquial) to twitch, to throb, to twinge
    • to move sharply, to jerk,
      • ло́шади дёрнули ― lóšadi djórnuli ― the horses gave a jerk
    • (colloquial) to disturb
      • (low colloquial) to drink, to knock back,
        • дёрнем ещё по рюмочке ― djórnem ješčó po rjúmočke ― let's knock back another one
