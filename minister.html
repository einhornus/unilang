minister (813)


Etymology

From minus + comparative suffix *-tero-. Compare magister.

Pronunciation

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /miˈnis.ter/, [mɪˈnɪs.t̪ɛr]
  • (Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /miˈnis.ter/, [miˈnis.t̪ɛr]

Noun

minister m (genitive ministrī); second declension

  1. attendant, servant, waiter
  2. agent, aide
  3. accomplice
