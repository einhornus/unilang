кати́ть -> to roll (sth)
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: c

Я качу́
Ты ка́тишь
Past: кати́л/кати́ла



Etymology: Inherited from Old East Slavic катити (katiti), from Proto-Slavic *kotiti



Definitions (English):
  • to roll, to push (some object with wheels)
    • кати́ть бо́чку ― katítʹ bóčku ― turn in, blow the whistle
  • (colloquial) to drive (quickly)
    • (a car etc.) to bowl along
