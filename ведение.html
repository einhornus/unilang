ведение (7285)


Etymology 1

From ведать (védatʹ, “to know, to be in charge”) +‎ -ение (-enije), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *weyd-.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈvʲedʲɪnʲɪje]

Noun

ведение • (védenije) n inan (genitive ведения, nominative plural ведения, genitive plural ведений)

  1. knowledge, lore
  2. authority, charge, competence
Related terms
  • краеве́дческий (krajevédčeskij)

Etymology 2

From вед- (ved-, “to conduct, direct”) +‎ -ение (-énije), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *weǵʰ- (“to move”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [vʲɪˈdʲenʲɪje]

Noun

ведение • (vedénije) n inan (genitive ведения, nominative plural ведения, genitive plural ведений)

  1. directing, leadership
    ведение (бухга́лтерских) кни́г ― vedenije (bugálterskix/buhálterskix) knig ― bookkeeping
    ведение хозя́йства ― vedenije xozjájstva ― housekeeping
    ведение огня́ ― vedenije ognjá ― firing (weapons)
  2. management, managing
  3. conducting
