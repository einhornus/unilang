багрове́ть (19002)


Etymology

From Church Slavonic">Old Church Slavonic багрити (bagriti, “to redden, to become scarlet-coloured”), from Church Slavonic">Old Church Slavonic багръ (bagrŭ, “purple”), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *bʰeh₁- (“to heat up; bathe”). Cognates include English bake, German bähen (“to bake”) and Ancient Greek φώγω (phṓgō, “to roast”)

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [bəɡrɐˈvʲetʲ]

Verb

багрове́ть • (bagrovétʹ) impf (perfective побагрове́ть)

  1. to grow/turn crimson/purple, to redden, to flush
    багрове́ть от́ гне́ва ― bagrovétʹ ot gnéva ― flush with anger; turn purple with rage

Related terms

  • багро́вый (bagróvyj)
  • багрянец (bagrjanec)
  • багря́ный (bagrjányj)
