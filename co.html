co (3603)


Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *ḱóm (“next to, at, with, alonǵ”). Cognate with German ge- (“with”) (collective prefix) and gegen (“toward, against́”), English gain-, Spanish con (“with”), Russian ко (ko, “to”).

Adverb

co

  1. how?
    Co·bbia mo ḟechtas? ― How will my expedition be?

Usage notes

Is followed by the dependent form of the verb, which is neither nasalized nor lenited.

Preposition

co

  1. to, toward
