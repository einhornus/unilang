косо́й -> slanting, oblique, squint, cross-eyed, squinting
Adjective



Etymology: Per Vasmer, derived from коса́ (kosá, “plait, braid”). Cognate with Ukrainian косий (kósyj), Serbo-Croatian ко̑с, Czech kosý, Polish kosy.



Definitions (English):
  • slanting, oblique, skewed (inclined at an angle to the plane not straight, not sheer)
    • twisted, wry, crooked
      • to the side, not centered
        • (figuratively) distrustful, suspicious, unfriendly (gaze, glance, etc.)
          • strabismic, suffering from strabismus, cross-eyed, squinting
            • (colloquial) cross-eyed person
              • (colloquial) hare
                • (slang) drunk (police slang)
                  • instrumental singular of коса́ (kosá)
                    • genitive singular of коса́я (kosája)
                      • dative singular of коса́я (kosája)
                        • instrumental singular of коса́я (kosája)
                          • prepositional singular of коса́я (kosája)
