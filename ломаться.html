лома́ться -> to break, (coll) put on airs, (coll) be awkward, make difficulties
Verb, imperfective, intransitive, stress pattern: a

Я лома́юсь
Ты лома́ешься
Past: лома́лся/лома́лась



Etymology: лома́ть (lomátʹ) +‎ -ся (-sja)



Definitions (English):
  • (intransitive) to break, to break up, to fracture, to split
    • (intransitive) to break down (of a device, etc.)
      • (figuratively) to collapse, to fall apart
        • to crack, to break (of the voice)
          • (colloquial) to pose, to mince, to pretend. Perfective:
            • поломаться (polomátʹsja) (only)
          • (colloquial) to be obstinate, to cause difficulties, to refuse to agree.
            • Он до́лго лома́лся, но потом мы его́ уговори́ли пойти́ с на́ми. On dólgo lomálsja, no potóm my jevó ugovoríli pojtí s námi. He was pigheaded for a long time but then we convinced him to come with us. Perfective: поломаться (polomátʹsja) (only)
          • passive of лома́ть (lomátʹ)
