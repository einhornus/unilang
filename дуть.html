дуть -> to blow
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: a

Я ду́ю
Ты ду́ешь
Past: ду́л/ду́ла



Etymology: From Late Proto-Slavic *dǫti from Early Proto-Slavic *dъm-ti, from the root of *dymъ (“smoke”). Cognate to Russian дым (dym) and English damp.



Definitions (English):
  • to blow.
    • Ветер ду́ет нам в лицо́. Véter dújet nam v licó. The wind is blowing into our faces.
  • to be drafty.
    • Здесь ду́ет. Zdesʹ dújet. There is a draft here.
  • to go quickly, to scamper.
    • А ну ду́й отсю́да! A nu duj otsjúda! Off with you quickly!
