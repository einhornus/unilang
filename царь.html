царь -> tsar
Noun, declension 2b (pattern язы́к)

Plural: цари́
Genitive: царя́



Etymology: From Old East Slavic цьсарь (cĭsarĭ), from цѣсарь (cěsarĭ), from Proto-Slavic *cěsařь, from a Germanic language, from Proto-Germanic *kaisaraz, from Latin Caesar.



Definitions (English):
  • tsar, Russian emperor
    • king (figuratively, or referring to ancient or non-European monarchs)
      • царь звере́й ― carʹ zveréj ― king of beasts, царь и бог ― carʹ i box ― God Almighty, царь небе́сный ― carʹ nebésnyj ― Heavenly Father, царь царе́й ― carʹ caréj ― King of kings, при царе́ Горо́хе ― pri caré Goróxe ― in the year dot a very long time ago since the beginning. Он без царя́ в голове́! ― On bez carjá v golové! ― He’s stupid!
