чиха́ть -> to sneeze
Verb, imperfective, intransitive, stress pattern: a

Я чиха́ю
Ты чиха́ешь
Past: чиха́л/чиха́ла



Etymology: Inherited from Proto-Slavic *čixati, of onomatopoeic origin. Cognate with Bulgarian чихамь (číhamʹ) (Gerov), Slovene číhati (tonal orthography) (1sg. čȋham (tonal orthography)), Upper Sorbian čichać, Lower Sorbian tśichaś. Also found as чхать (čxatʹ), inherited from Proto-Slavic *čьxati, cognate with Ukrainian чхати (čxáty), Belarusian чхаць (čxacʹ), Polish czchać.



Definitions (English):
  • to sneeze
    • to scorn, to disdain,
      • мне чиха́ть (на)mne čixátʹ (na) I don't give a damn (for), I don't care a button (about)
