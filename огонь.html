ого́нь -> fire, light
Noun, declension 2b (pattern язы́к)

Plural: огни́
Genitive: огня́



Etymology: From Old East Slavic огнь (ognĭ), from Proto-Slavic *ogňь, from Proto-Balto-Slavic *ugnis, from Proto-Indo-European *h₁n̥gʷnis, whence English ignition and igneous. Cognate with Latvian uguns, Latin ignis, Sanskrit अग्नि (agní). Compare also Hittite 𒀀𒀝𒉌𒅖 (a-ak-ni-iš), an Indo-Aryan borrowing.
