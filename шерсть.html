шерсть -> wool, hair (of animal)
Noun, declension 3e (pattern ночь)

Plural: ше́рсти
Genitive: ше́рсти



Etymology: From Old East Slavic сьрсть (sĭrstĭ), (since the 14th century) шьрсть (šĭrstĭ), from Proto-Slavic *sьrstь, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱer(s)- (“rough hair, bristle”) + *-tь, whence also Lithuanian šerys, English hair.



Definitions (English):
  • fur, hair (animal hair or human body hair contrast
    • во́лосы and мех)
  • wool (hair of an animal used to make clothing)
    • woollen cloth, worsted, wadding
