па́рус -> sail
Noun, declension 2c (pattern го́род)

Plural: паруса
Genitive: паруса



Etymology: From Old East Slavic парусъ (parusŭ), of uncertain origin. Either to be connected to Livonian pūŗaz (“sail”), Lithuanian bura (“sail”), Lithuanian bùrė (“sail”), or borrowed from Ancient Greek φᾶρος, φάρος (phâros, pháros, “large cloth, spread”), for which latter compare уксус (úksus, “vinegar”) and пардус (párdus, “pard”), known Greek borrowings.
