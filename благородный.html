благоро́дный -> noble
Adjective



Etymology: Borrowed from Old Church Slavonic благородьнъ (blagorodĭnŭ). Synchronically analyzable as бла́го (blágo, “good”) +‎ -о- (-o-) +‎ род (rod, “birth”) +‎ -ный (-nyj).



Definitions (English):
  • noble, aristocratic (of the nobility, of the high-born class, especially in Russia before 1917)
    • noble (in spirit), gentle,
      • благоро́дный посту́пок ― blagoródnyj postúpok ― noble act
    • selfless, generous
      • exceptional, outstandingly elegant,
        • благоро́дная красота́ ― blagoródnaja krasotá ― exceptional beauty
      • (sciences, figuratively) noble (having some special quality)
        • благоро́дный газ ― blagoródnyj gáz ― noble gas, inert gas
      • noble, aristocrat (member of the nobility, of the high-born class, especially in Russia before 1917)
        • someone noble in spirit
