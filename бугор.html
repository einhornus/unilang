буго́р (6248)


Etymology

Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *bʰewgʰ- (“to bend”). Cognates include Ukrainian бугір (buhír), Lithuanian baũgurs.

Non-Slavic cognates include Sanskrit भुग्न (bhugna, “curved, crooked”), Latin fugio (“I flee, I hasten”), English bow

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [bʊˈɡor]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

буго́р • (bugór) m inan or m anim (genitive бугра́, nominative plural бугры́, genitive plural бугро́в)

  1. (inanimate) hill, hillock
    Synonyms: курга́н (kurgán), холм (xolm)
  2. (inanimate) bump
    Synonyms: ко́чка (kóčka), шишка (šíška)
  3. (animate, colloquial) big cheese, boss
