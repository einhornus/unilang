выводи́ть -> to lead out, remove
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: c

Я вывожу́
Ты выво́дишь
Past: выводи́л/выводи́ла



Etymology: вы- (vý-) +‎ води́ть (vodítʹ)



Definitions (English):
  • to take out, to lead out, to bring out, to move out to help out (help somebody to get off)
    • выводи́ть на чи́стую во́ду ― vyvodítʹ na čístuju vódu ― to bring out into the open, to expose, to unmask, to show up
  • to withdraw, to call off (troops)
    • to derive, to conclude, to infer, to deduce,
      • выводи́ть фо́рмулу ― vyvodítʹ fórmulu ― to deduce / derive a formula
    • to remove,
      • выводи́ть из соста́ва прези́диума ― vyvodítʹ iz sostáva prezídiuma ― to remove from the presidium, выводи́ть из себя́ ― vyvodítʹ iz sebjá ― to drive smb. out of his wits, to drive smb. to distraction to drive smb. crazy (literally, “to remove (someone) from himself/herself”) Э́та про́бка выво́дит меня из себя́ ― Éta próbka vyvódit menjá iz sebjá ― This traffic jam is driving me crazy.выводи́ть из терпе́ния ― vyvodítʹ iz terpénija ― to make smb. lose patience, to exasperate smb. (literally, “to remove (someone) from patience”)выводи́ть из стро́я ― vyvodítʹ iz strója ― to put out of action / operation / commission, to disable, to wreck (literally, “to remove from the order”)
    • to extirpate, to destroy to exterminate (pests) to remove (stains, pimples)
