es (8160)


Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch essche, from Old Dutch *aska, from Proto-Germanic *askaz, *askiz (compare West Frisian esk, English ash, German Esche, Danish ask), from Proto-Indo-European *h₃osk- (compare Welsh onnen, Latin ornus (“wild mountain ash”), Lithuanian úosis, Russian я́сень (jasenʹ), Albanian ah (“beech”), Ancient Greek ὀξύα (oxúa, “beech”), Old Armenian հացի (hacʿi)).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ɛs/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: es
  • Rhymes: -ɛs

Noun

  1. ash, ash tree
