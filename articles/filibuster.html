filibuster (12626)


ед. ч. мн. ч.
filibuster filibusters

fi-li-bus-ter

Существи́тельное.

Ко́рень: -filibuster-.

Значе́ние

  1. полит. филибастер
    The filibuster deployed a variety of procedural delays — including the filing of successive censure motions against the government — and employed the delaying tactic of ‘ox-walking’ when voting on them. «Keesings Contemporary Archive», 1985–1994 гг. (Цита́та взята́ из BYU-BNC British National Corpus, см. Спи́сок литерату́ры)
  2. наёмник, вооружённый авантюри́ст
    Filibusters are irregular soldiers who (normally) act without official authority from their own government, and are generally motivated by financial gain, political ideology, or the thrill of adventure. «Filibuster (military)» (цита́та из Википедии, см. Спи́сок литерату́ры)
  3. истор. флибустьер
    This island, at the time I mention, was blockaded by that notorious filibuster, Reginald Bendbrisket, a rogue who, possessed of the greatest audacity and cunning, had earned for himself an unenviable reputation, from Margate to Samoa, by the terrible extent of his depredations. William Heath Robinson, «Bill the Minder», 1912 г. (цита́та из Викитеки)

Сино́нимы

  1. —
  2. mercenary, irregular, freebooter
  3. flibustier, freebooter, buccaneer, pirate
