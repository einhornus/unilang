све́рстник (8489)


Etymology

Inherited from Old East Slavic съвьрстьникъ (sŭvĭrstĭnikŭ). Compare Old East Slavic съвьрстьнъ (sŭvĭrstĭnŭ, “of the same age”), съвьрсть (sŭvĭrstĭ, “of the same age; coeval, age-mate”), from вьрста (vĭrsta, “age”). Compare Slovene vȓst (“row, type, age”) (tonal orthography) (genitive vrstȋ (tonal orthography)), Sanskrit वृत्ति f (vṛttí, “method, essence, nature, gender”). Morphologically с- (s-) + верста́ (verstá) + -ник (-nik).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈsvʲers⁽ʲ⁾nʲɪk]

Noun

све́рстник • (svérstnik) m anim (genitive све́рстника, nominative plural све́рстники, genitive plural све́рстников, feminine све́рстница)

  1. contemporary
  2. coeval
  3. age-mate (one who is the same age as another)
