хала́т (2126)


Etymology

A Turkic borrowing. Compare Ottoman Turkish خلعت (hilat, “kaftan”), Azerbaijani xələt (“dressing gown”), Uzbek xalat (“robe”). Ultimately from Arabic خِلْعَة (ḵilʿa, “robe of honor”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [xɐˈlat]
  • audio
    		(file)

Noun

хала́т • (xalát) m inan (genitive хала́та, nominative plural хала́ты, genitive plural хала́тов)

  1. dressing gown, bathrobe (for men or women)
  2. dress (any kind that is knee-length to full and that opens down the front)
  3. smock
  4. surgical gown
  5. oriental robe

Derived terms

  • хала́тный (xalátnyj)
