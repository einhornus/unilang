фля́жка (11573)


Etymology

From older фляшка (fljaška), borrowed from a Germanic language, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *flaskǭ. Compare Old High German flasca (Modern German Flasche), English flask.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈflʲaʂkə]

Noun

фля́жка • (fljážka) f inan (genitive фля́жки, nominative plural фля́жки, genitive plural фля́жек)

  1. flask, hip flask (container for a small amount of beverage)

Derived terms

  • фля́га (fljága) (by back-formation)
  • пло́ская фля́жка f (plóskaja fljážka)
