со́шка (23478)


Etymology

соха́ (soxá) + -ка (-ka)

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈsoʂkə]

Noun

со́шка • (sóška) f inan (genitive со́шки, nominative plural со́шки, genitive plural со́шек)

  1. endearing diminutive of соха́ (soxá, “plow”)
  2. (automobile) steering joint
  3. (military) bipod (mount for a weapon)
  4. (photography) monopod
  5. (dated, military) stand with a horizontal bar for installing guns

Noun

со́шка • (sóška) f anim (genitive со́шки, nominative plural со́шки, genitive plural со́шек)

  1. (dated) unimportant person, nonentity, sideman
    • 2018, Кровосток (lyrics and music), “столя́рка”, in ЧБ:
      да́же мел́кие со́шки от́ стаме́ски кобзо́ном ору́т
      Dáže meĺkije sóški ot staméski Kobzónom orút
      Even the trivial sidemen cry after Kobzon from the chisel

Related terms

  • соха́ (soxá), со́шный (sóšnyj)
  • сошни́к (sošník)
