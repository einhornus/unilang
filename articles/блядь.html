блядь (1209)


Etymology

From Old East Slavic блѧдь (blędĭ), from Proto-Slavic *blędь (“error”), from *blęsti (“to talk nonsense, err”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [blʲætʲ]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

блядь • (bljadʹ) f anim (genitive бля́ди, nominative plural бля́ди, genitive plural бляде́й)

  1. (vulgar) whore, slut, prostitute
    ходи́ть по блядя́м ― xodítʹ po bljadjám ― to go whoring
    за́сраная блядь ― zásranaja bljadʹ ― stinking whore
  2. (vulgar) bitch, slut, promiscuous person
  3. (vulgar) general-purpose insult — compare shithead, bastard, etc.

Synonyms

  • In order of increasing vulgarity:
    • проститу́тка (prostitútka)
    • шлю́ха (šljúxa)
    • потаску́ха (potaskúxa)
    • ку́рва (kúrva)

Related terms

  • блядивый (bljadivyj)
  • блядоход (bljadoxod)
  • бля́дский (bljádskij)
  • бля́дство (bljádstvo)
  • блясти (bljasti)

Interjection

блядь • (bljadʹ)

  1. (vulgar) interjection (may be contracted to бля (blja)); compare for fuck's sake !, shit!, etc.
    • Ты что де́лаешь, блядь?!
      Ty što délaješʹ, bljadʹ?!
      What are you doing, for fuck's sake?!
  2. (vulgar, intensive) (may be contracted to бля (blja)); compare damn, fucking, etc.
    бля бу́ду ― blja búdu ― I’ll be a whore (used as an affirmation; translates to "I understand what you expect of me and I take full responsibility for what I said and I swear that what I said is the truth".)
    • Бля бу́ду, не бра́л я твои́ штаны́!
      Blja búdu, ne bral ja tvoí štaný!
      I fucking swear, I didn't take your pants!
  3. (vulgar) discourse particle
    Я, бля, три дня не пил! ― Ja, blja, tri dnja ne pil! ― I haven't had a drink in three fuckin' days!
