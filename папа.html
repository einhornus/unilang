па́па -> dad, Pope
Noun, declension 1a
Plural: па́пы
Genitive: па́пы



Etymologies:
  • A Lallwort. Perhaps borrowed from French papa obsolete variant па́па is certainly French.
  • From Latin pāpa (“pope”), from Greek originally a Lallwort. Doublet of поп (pop).
  • A Lallwort perhaps of native origin. Many other similar examples are present cross-linguistically see the entry for English pap for more.


Definitions (English):
  • dad, daddy
    • (tech) male socket
      • pope
        • (archaic, childish, dialectal) bread, especially white bread
          • (archaic, childish, dialectal) food
