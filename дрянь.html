дрянь -> rubbish (coll)
Noun, declension 3a (pattern жзнь)

Plural: дрни
Genitive: дрни



Etymology: First attested in 1731. From дрань (dranʹ), from Proto-Slavic *dьranь, from *dьranъ, past passive participle of *dьrati (“to tear, flay”).



Definitions (English):
  • (colloquial) rubbish, trash1929 – 1940,
    • М. А. Булгаков, Ма́стер и Маргарита :Пошёл я купа́ться на Москва-реку, ну и попятили мою одёжу, а э́ту дрянь оста́вили! Pošól ja kupátʹsja na Moskvá-réku, nu i popjátili móju odjóžu, a étu drjanʹ ostávili!1979 translation by Richard Pevear, Larissa Volokhonsky. I went for a swim in the Moscow River, so they filched my clothes and left me this trash!
  • (figuratively, colloquial, animate) rotter, skunk, dirty dog
