Noun[edit]

корчма́ • (korčmá) f inan (genitive корчмы́, nominative plural корчмы́, genitive plural корче́м, diminutive корчомка)

  1. (dated) tavern, inn where spirits are sold (in Ukraine or Belarus before the Russian Revolution)
    Synonyms: каба́к (kabák), пивна́я (pivnája), шинок (šinók), бар (bar), паб (pab), тракти́р (traktír), харче́вня (xarčévnja)

Usage notes[edit]

  • Historically, there was a legal distinction between a private корчма́ (korčmá) or шинок (šinók) serving tax-free spirits, and a taxed, government-licensed каба́к (kabák).
