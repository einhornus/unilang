трави́ть -> to exterminate, poison, damage, hunt, persecute
Verb, imperfective, intransitive, stress pattern: c

Я травлю́
Ты тра́вишь
Past: трави́л/трави́ла



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *traviti. Cognate with Old Church Slavonic травити (traviti), Ukrainian травити (travýty), Serbo-Croatian травити (first singular тра̑ви̑м), Czech tráviti, Slovak trávit᾽, Polish trawić (first singular trawię). Further cognate with Ancient Greek τρώω (trṓō, “to hurt”), τιτρώσκω (titrṓskō), τρύ̄ω (trú̄ō), Old High German drawa (“threat”), drōа (“threat”), Old English þrēа (“threat, punishment”). More distantly related to тере́ть (terétʹ, “to rub”).

  • (colloquial) to tell,
    • трави́ть анекдо́ты ― travítʹ anekdóty ― to tell jokes, трави́ть байки ― travítʹ bájki ― to tell tall tales
