попра́ть (14237)


Etymology

From Old East Slavic попьрати (popĭrati), borrowed from Church Slavonic">Old Church Slavonic попьрати (popĭrati), composed as if from по- (po-) +‎ прать (pratʹ, “to crush, to stomp; to go against́, to resist”), from Proto-Slavic *pьrati; cognate with попере́ть (poperétʹ).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [pɐˈpratʲ]

Verb

попра́ть • (poprátʹ) pf (imperfective попира́ть)

  1. to violate, to humiliate
  2. (dated) to trample
