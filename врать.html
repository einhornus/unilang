врать -> to tell lies, talk nonsense
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: b/c

Я вру́
Ты врёшь
Past: вра́л/врала́



Etymology: From Proto-Slavic *vьrati, 1sg. *vьrǫ (as a verb, only preserved in Russian and Serbo-Croatian). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *werh₁- (“to speak”). Cognate with Serbo-Croatian varati (“to betray”), Lithuanian var̃das (“name”), Latvian vārds (“name word”), Latin verbum (“word”), English word, Ancient Greek εἴρω (eírō, “to speak”), ῥήτωρ (rhḗtōr, “orator”) (compare English rhetoric, borrowed from Greek), Hittite [script needed] (weriya-, “to call, to name”), Sanskrit व्रत (vratá, “pledge, vow”).



Definitions (English):
  • to lie
    • to make a mistake
      • to be inaccurate
        • to tell tales
