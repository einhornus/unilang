шерша́вый -> rough (to the touch)
Adjective



Etymology: From шерша́в (šeršáv, “rough”) +‎ -вый (-vyj), which per Vasmer comes either from Old East Slavic шьрсть (šĭrstĭ) -and is thus cognate to шерсть (šerstʹ, “wool”)- or from a root related to шо́рох (šórox, “rustle, murmur”). Compare Ukrainian шерехатий (šerexátyj), Bulgarian шерхаць (šérhacʹ), Slovene sŕšati



Definitions (English):
  • rough, coarse
