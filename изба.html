изба́ -> peasant cottage

Etymology: From Old East Slavic изъба, истъба (izŭba, istŭba), from Proto-Slavic *jьstъba, which may be related to Proto-Germanic *stubō (“room, sitting room, oven”) or to étuve (“heated room, bathroom”), itself from Latin *extupāre.



Definitions (English):
  • izba, (peasant's) log hut, cottage,
    • вы́носить сор из избы ― vynosítʹ sor iz izbý ― to wash one’s dirty linen in public
