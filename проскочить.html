проскочи́ть -> to rush past, slip through
Verb, perfective, transitive, stress pattern: c

Я проскочу́
Ты проско́чишь
Past: проскочи́л/проскочи́ла



Etymology: про- (pro-) +‎ скочить (skočítʹ)



Definitions (English):
  • (colloquial) to rush, to dart, to dash, to fly, to shoot
    • (colloquial) to go (past), to leave (behind), to cross, to make it (across)
      • маши́на едва́ проскочи́ла перекрёсток ― mašína jedvá proskočíla perekrjóstok ― the car just barely crossed the intersection
    • (colloquial) to miss, to overshoot,
      • проскочи́ть свою́ остано́вку ― proskočítʹ svojú ostanóvku ― to miss one's stop, проскочи́ть поворо́т с магистра́ли ― proskočítʹ povorót s magistráli ― to miss/overshoot the exit from the highway
    • (colloquial) to fall (through, between)
      • (colloquial) to slip in, to sneak in, to slip (into), to sneak (into)
        • (colloquial) to manage to become (smb), to manage to get (somewhere)
          • проскочи́ть в нача́льники ― proskočítʹ v načálʹniki ― to manage to become a boss, проскочи́ть в университе́т ― proskočítʹ v universitét ― to manage to enter the university, проскочи́ть в дамки (о ша́шке) ― proskočítʹ v dámki (o šáške) ― to manage to get crowned
        • (colloquial) to make it (through), to manage, to cope (with), to hurdle,
          • сам не зна́ю, как я проскочи́л (че́рез) экза́мен ― sam ne znáju, kak ja proskočíl (čérez) ekzámen ― I don't know myself how I made it through the exam
        • (colloquial) to slip out,
          • как э́та информа́ция могла́ проскочи́ть в пре́ссу? ― kak éta informácija moglá proskočítʹ v préssu? ― how could this information have leaked [slipped out] into the press?проскочи́ло мно́го оши́бок ― proskočílo mnógo ošíbok ― many errors have crept / slipped in
        • (colloquial) to slip by, to pass quickly,
          • ле́то проскочи́ло незаме́тно ― léto proskočílo nezamétno ― the summer slipped by before we [they, etc] knew it
