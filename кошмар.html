кошмар -> nightmare
Noun, declension 2a (pattern мальчик)

Plural: кошмары
Genitive: кошмара



Etymology: Connected to French cauchemar, from Middle French cauchemare, from Old French cauquemare. First element from Latin calcare (“I trample, tread on”) second element from Middle Dutch mare (“phantom, spirit, nightmare”), from Proto-Germanic *marǭ (“nightmare, incubus”), from Proto-Indo-European *mer- (“to die”). See cauchemar.
