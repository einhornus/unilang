шабаш (18020)


Etymology

From Polish language">Polish szabas from Yiddish שבת‎ (shabes, “Sabbath”), from Hebrew שַׁבָּת‎ (shabát). Cognate to Russian суббо́та (subbóta).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈʂabəʂ]
  • IPA(key): [ʂɐˈbaʂ]

Noun

шабаш or шабаш • (šábaš or šabáš) masculine gender">m inan (genitive шабаша or шабаша, nominative plural шабаши or шабаши, genitive plural шабашей or шабашей)

  1. Saturday, observed in Judaism as a day of rest, Sabbath.
  2. A meeting of witches, presided over by the devil, Sabbath.
  3. Any merry debauched festival.
  4. (colloquial) end of the working day, quitting time (also pronounced šabáš).
    Шабаш, охо́тники! Сла́вно порабо́тали! — ве́село кри́кнул де́душка. — Сейча́с, пожа́луй, и домо́й пора.
    Šabaš, oxótniki! Slávno porabótali! — véselo kríknul déduška. — Sejčás, požáluj, i domój porá.
    "That’s it, hunters! We did a great job!" the grandfather shouted merrily. "Now it’s about time we went home."

Related terms

  • шаба́шник (šabašnik)
  • шабашка (šabaška)
  • шабашничать (šabašničatʹ)
