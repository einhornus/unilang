тро́гать -> to touch
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: a

Я тро́гаю
Ты тро́гаешь
Past: тро́гал/тро́гала



Etymology: From the same root as Latin trahō according to Goryaev, which would be Proto-Indo-European *tregʰ-, whence English drag, treat, trace, and traction.



Definitions (English):
  • to touch
    • to move (emotionally)
      • to bother, to annoy, to disturb
        • (intransitive) to start moving
