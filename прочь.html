прочь (2992)


Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [prot͡ɕ]
  • Rhymes: -ot͡ɕ

Etymology 1

Inherited from Old East Slavic прочь (pročĭ, “awaý”). Cognate with Old East Slavic проче (proče, “except́”), Ukrainian пріч (prič, “awaý”), прочанин (pročányn, “pilgrim”), Belarusian проч (proč, “awaý”), Church Slavonic">Old Church Slavonic прочь (pročĭ, “rest, remaining”), проче (proče, “therefore”), Bulgarian прочее (próčee, “therefore”), Slovene pròč (“awaý”) (tonal orthography), Polish language">Polish oprócz (“except́”); also, less clearly, with Czech pryč (“awaý, out”), Slovak preč (“awaý”), Polish language">Polish precz (“awaý”), Upper Sorbian language">Upper Sorbian preč (“awaý”), Lower Sorbian language">Lower Sorbian pšec (“awaý”) (also pšejc). Per Vasmer, apparently an original comparative of Proto-Slavic *prokъ (whence Russian прок (prok)), originally an adjective meaning "ahead". See Russian про́чий (próčij) and прок (prok) for more information.

Adverb

прочь • (pročʹ)

  1. awaý, aside

Interjection

прочь • (pročʹ)

  1. be off, get out
  2. begone, awaý with you
  3. beat it, scat
  4. hence
Related terms
  • про́чий (próčij)

Etymology 2

Verb

прочь • (pročʹ)

  1. second-person singular imperative imperfective of про́чить (próčitʹ)

прочь

Наре́чие, обстоятельственное, места, также междоме́тие; неизменяемое.

Ко́рень: -прочь- [Тихонов, 1996].
