ры́нда (30574)


Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈrɨndə]

Etymology 1

Noun

ры́нда • (rýnda) m anim (genitive ры́нды, nominative plural ры́нды, genitive plural ры́нд)

  1. (historical) an armor-bearer or bodyguard of a Muscovite tsar
Synonyms
  • (armor-bearer): оружено́сец (oruženósec)
  • (bodyguard): телохрани́тель (teloxranítelʹ)

Etymology 2

From a mondegreen of English “ring the bell”, a British officer's command interpreted as «ры́нду бей» (“rýndu bej”, “strike the rynda”) by Russian sailors.

Noun

ры́нда • (rýnda) f inan (genitive ры́нды, nominative plural ры́нды, genitive plural ры́нд)

  1. (nautical) three triple strikes of a ship's bell marking noon in Russian naval tradition
  2. (informal) a ship's bell
Synonyms
  • (ship's bell): судово́й ко́локол (sudovój kólokol), корабе́льный ко́локол (korabélʹnyj kólokol)
