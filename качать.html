кача́ть -> to rock, shake, swing
Verb, imperfective, transitive, stress pattern: a

Я кача́ю
Ты кача́ешь
Past: кача́л/кача́ла



Etymology: Inherited from Proto-Slavic *kačati, iterative-durative for *kotiti (“to roll”).



Definitions (English):
  • to rock, to swing, to shake, to wobble to dandle,
    • кача́ть колыбе́ль ― kačátʹ kolybélʹ ― to rock the cradle, кача́ть голово́й ― kačátʹ golovój ― to shake one's head
  • to pump
    • to toss, to roll, to pitch,
      • ло́дку кача́ет ― lódku kačájet ― the boat is tossing / rolling, его́ кача́ло из стороны в сто́рону ― jevó kačálo iz storoný v stóronu ― he was reeling from side to side
    • (colloquial) to lift up, to chair (to toss someone up, like a group of fans their champion)
      • to download. Perfective:
        • скачать (skačátʹ) Synonym: скачивать (skáčivatʹ)
      • (colloquial) to train/pump muscle
        • (colloquial) (in the expression)
          • кача́ть права ― kačátʹ pravá ― to (persist, е, ntly) stand up for one's rights
