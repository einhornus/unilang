горожа́нин (4484)


Etymology

From Old East Slavic <small>[Term?], inherited from Proto-Slavic *gorďaninъ. Doublet of граждани́н (graždanín), a borrowing from Old Church Slavonic.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ɡərɐˈʐanʲɪn]
  • Audio
    		(file)

Noun

горожа́нин • (gorožánin) m anim (genitive горожа́нина, nominative plural горожа́не, genitive plural горожа́н, feminine горожанка)

  1. urbanite, townsman (male resident of a town)

Related terms

  • го́род m (górod)
