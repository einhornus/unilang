шрам -> scar
Noun, declension 2a (pattern ма́льчик)

Plural: шра́мы
Genitive: шра́ма



Etymology: From Ukrainian шрам (šram), from Polish szram, szrama, from Middle High German schramme, schramm (German Schramme).



Definitions (English):
  • scar, cicatrix (a permanent mark on the skin sometimes caused by the healing of a wound)
