Etymology

[1589] Borrowed from Middle French">Middle French cavalier (“horseman”),[1] itself borrowed from Old Italian cavaliere (“mounted soldier, knight”),[2], borrowed from Old Occitan pro">cavalier, from Late Latin caballārius (“horseman”), from Latin caballus (“horse”), probably from Gaulish caballos 'nag', variant of cabillos (compare Welsh ceffyl, Breton kefel, Irish capall), akin to German (Swabish) Kōb 'nag' and Old Church Slavonic kobyla 'mare'.

Previous English forms include cavalero and cavaliero.
