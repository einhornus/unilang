ба́бочка -> butterfly
Noun, declension 1a (pattern де́вочка)

Plural: ба́бочки
Genitive: ба́бочки



Etymology: ба́бка (bábka, “old woman, grandmother”) +‎ -ка (-ka), originally a diminutive from the folk belief that spirits of the dead live on as butterflies.



Definitions (English):
  • butterfly,
    • ночна́я ба́бочка ― nočnája bábočka ― moth
  • bowtie
    • (colloquial) feisty, spirited woman
      • (colloquial) prostitute, hooker
