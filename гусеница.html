гу́сеница -> caterpillar
Noun, declension 1a (pattern де́вочка)

Plural: гу́сеницы
Genitive: гу́сеницы



Etymology: Cognate with Old Church Slavonic юсеница (jusenica), Ukrainian гусениця (húsenycja), Bulgarian гъсеница (gǎsénica), Serbo-Croatian гу̏сеница/gȕsenica/гу̏сјеница/gȕsjenica, Slovene gosẹ̑nica, Czech housenka, Slovak husenica, Old Polish wąsienica, Polish gąsienica, Upper Sorbian husańca, Lower Sorbian guseńca. Probably from Proto-Slavic *ǫsěnica. The initial г (g)- is prothetic or by conflation with Common Slavonic гоущерица (gušterica, “lizard”) (Russian ящерица (jáščerica)), and is not related to Russian гусь (gusʹ).



Definitions (English):
  • caterpillar
    • (mechanical) caterpillar track
